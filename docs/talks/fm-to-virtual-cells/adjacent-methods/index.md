# Adjacent methods — algorithm papers next to the talk's topic

> *Resource section for the [FM-to-Virtual-Cells talk](../../fm-to-virtual-cells.md). The talk is about foundation models, but the questions it cares about — perturbation prediction, generative cell modelling, cheap model adaptation, the architecture debate — are also worked on by people **not** building FMs. These pages are dossiers for those algorithm and review papers: not part of the FM corpus proper, but close enough to the big topic that they belong on the shelf.*

Each page answers the same three things: **what the method is**, **how it connects to the talk's argument**, and **where to read more**. They are deliberately *not* foundation-model papers — that is the point of keeping them in their own section rather than the [model glossary](../model-glossary.md) or the [paper map](../paper-map.md).

## How this section relates to the rest of the talk prep

- The [model glossary](../model-glossary.md) and [paper map](../paper-map.md) cover the **foundation models** and the literature *about* them.
- This section covers **methods next door** — algorithm papers and reviews that inform the same problems without being FM papers themselves.
- Anything here that turns out to be load-bearing for the argument graduates into the paper map; until then it lives here as a resource.
