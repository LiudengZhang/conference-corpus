# David L. Rimoin Lifetime Achievement Award — John C. Carey

**The ACMG Foundation's highest honor goes to John C. Carey, MD, MPH, FACMG, FAAP — Professor Emeritus of Pediatrics at the University of Utah and a >40-year career architect of clinical genetics, dysmorphology, and trisomy 13/18 patient advocacy.**

## Headline finding

Carey receives the **2026 David L. Rimoin Lifetime Achievement Award** — the ACMG Foundation's highest honor, named for the founder of medical genetics as a clinical specialty. The citation recognizes a >40-year career spanning: creation/program-direction of the University of Utah Medical Genetics Training Program; >400 peer-reviewed articles; co-authorship of *Medical Genetics and Genomics* (now in its 6th edition); editorship of the *American Journal of Medical Genetics* (Editor-in-Chief, 2001–2016); and founding-professional-advisor role with SOFT, where Carey reshaped the clinical and ethical framing of trisomy 13 and 18 — moving the field from uniform non-intervention to individualized care.

## Methodology / framework

Carey's contribution is **clinical-genetics phenotype-first**: detailed dysmorphology, longitudinal natural-history studies of underserved rare-disease populations (trisomy 13/18 most prominently), and the training-pipeline architecture (>40 years of fellows and residents) that built much of the current academic-medical-genetics workforce.
