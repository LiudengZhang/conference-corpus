# Presidential Plenary: The Future of Expanded Newborn Genomic Screening

**ACMG 2026's opening plenary frames the policy + clinical-lab debate over moving newborn screening from biochemical panels to whole-genome sequencing — with the cancer-predisposition reporting wedge as a load-bearing sub-question.**

- **Speaker / institution:** Presidential Plenary panel (ACMG leadership + BabySeq2 / GUARDIAN / NBSeq / EarlyCheck program PIs); combined with ACMG Foundation Awards ceremony and Rimoin Lecture
- **Anchor type:** guideline + cohort
- **Day / session:** Day 2 (Wed Mar 11, 2026) — Presidential Plenary, morning, Baltimore Convention Center
- **Status:** post-meeting; from ACMG meeting site + PRNewswire pre-meeting press; per-talk speaker slate and concrete recommendations TBD pending GIM abstract supplement and Digital Edition recordings

## Headline finding

The plenary is the field's coordinating moment on **how, when, and with what consent framework** the four major US newborn-genomic-screening pilots (BabySeq2, GUARDIAN at Columbia, NBSeq, EarlyCheck) translate into a generalizable clinical-lab service. Specific cohort numerics (cumulative consented infants, additional-condition yield, false-positive rates per disorder, return-of-results uptake) TBD pending GIM supplement. The cancer-predisposition reporting wedge — whether RB1, TP53, RET, APC, BRCA1/2, MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2 variants should be reported from newborn WGS, and to whom — is treated as a discrete policy axis.

## Methodology / framework

Panel format combining cohort readouts (BabySeq2 randomized return-of-results trial; GUARDIAN's >100k-infant screening pilot; NBSeq; EarlyCheck) with framework discussion of the ACMG/AMP variant-classification thresholds appropriate for asymptomatic newborns, the role of the ACMG Secondary Findings v3.3 list, and gene-list selection (actionable pediatric-onset only, vs adult-onset cancer-predisposition genes reported deferred-to-adulthood vs reported-to-parents).

## Open questions

- Should adult-onset cancer-predisposition variants be reported from newborn WGS at all, and if so to parents or held to age 18?
- How does newborn WGS interact with the ACMG SF v3.3 list — is SF a floor, ceiling, or orthogonal?
- Payer coverage trajectory for newborn WGS in 2026–2028 — state-by-state vs federal
