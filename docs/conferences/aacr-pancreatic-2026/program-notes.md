## Conference chairs and stated programmatic priorities

Six co-chairs:

- **Andrew J. Aguirre** (Dana-Farber) — KRAS biology, functional genomics, PDX. Programmatic emphasis on KRAS-directed therapy and combination genomic screens.
- **Florencia McAllister** (MD Anderson) — microbiome, immune microenvironment, prevention / interception. Programmatic emphasis on tumor-microbiome cross-talk, early detection, and racial/ethnic disparities.
- **Kenneth P. Olive** (Columbia / Herbert Irving) — stroma biology, GEMMs, drug-delivery biology. Programmatic emphasis on the immunosuppressive microenvironment and stromal reprogramming.
- **Eileen O'Reilly** (MSK) — clinical pancreatic-cancer trials lead. Programmatic emphasis on Phase 2/3 readouts, novel agents in metastatic and adjuvant settings.
- **Diane M. Simeone** (UC San Diego) — early detection, cancer stem cells, host institution lead. Programmatic emphasis on biomarker development and high-risk cohort surveillance.
- **Colin D. Weekes** (Mass General / Harvard) — translational clinical, KRAS-directed trials. Programmatic emphasis on biomarker-driven Phase 1/2 design.

The co-chair composition signals a balanced KRAS-and-stroma-and-microenvironment-and-clinical program. Compared to AACR Pancreatic 2024 (where the KRAS / immunology axis was front-and-center for the sotorasib / adagrasib reveal cycle), 2026 likely tilts toward **(a)** Phase 2 maturation data for the pan-RAS / G12D-selective classes and **(b)** combination-trial design questions, while keeping the basic-biology and early-detection programs intact.

## Anticipated headline readouts

The five highest-priority watch items (per JPM 2026 sponsor guidance + Q1 2026 earnings + ASCO 2026 preview material):

### 1. Daraxonrasib (RMC-6236) Phase 2/3 in metastatic PDAC

- **Sponsor:** Revolution Medicines.
- **Trials:** RASolute-302 (Phase 3, 2L+ KRAS-mut metastatic PDAC, daraxonrasib mono vs SOC chemo); Phase 1b/2 combos with mFOLFIRINOX / gem-nab.
- **What we'd watch:** Magnitude of PFS / OS benefit in 2L+ monotherapy (Phase 3 may not read at this meeting — likelier ESMO 2026 / ASCO GI 2027 — but interim safety / pharmacodynamic updates are plausible). Combination Phase 1b/2 ORR / tolerability for 1L. AACR Pancreatic is the natural home for the depth-of-response / biomarker correlative story.
- **Stakes:** If PFS magnitude in 2L+ mono is clinically meaningful (HR <0.65), pan-RAS becomes the first practice-changing agent for the broad KRAS-mut PDAC population. *Pre-embargo: anticipated per sponsor guidance; final Phase 3 readout not certain at this venue.*

### 2. Zoldonrasib (RMC-9805) Phase 1 PDAC-specific data

- **Sponsor:** Revolution Medicines.
- **Trial:** First-in-human Phase 1 in KRAS-G12D solid tumors, with PDAC expansion cohort.
- **What we'd watch:** First PDAC-specific ORR / DLT / PK-PD in the G12D-selective subset (~40% of PDAC). Comparison to pan-RAS daraxonrasib in the same allele.
- **Stakes:** G12D-selective vs pan-RAS is the field's open question on tolerability vs efficacy trade-off in chronic-dosing PDAC management. *Pre-embargo: expected per sponsor guidance.*

### 3. BNT122 / autogene cevumeran Phase 2 randomized RFS

- **Sponsor:** BioNTech / Genentech / Memorial Sloan Kettering.
- **Trial:** IMcode-004 / NCT05968326 (Phase 2 randomized, resected PDAC, mRNA neoantigen vaccine + atezo + mFOLFIRINOX vs mFOLFIRINOX).
- **What we'd watch:** Whether the Phase 1 single-center RFS signal (Rojas et al. *Nature* 2023; mature follow-up ASCO 2024) replicates in a multi-center randomized Phase 2. T-cell persistence / immunogenicity correlatives. AACR Pancreatic is the natural home for the deep-immune-correlate story.
- **Stakes:** If RFS benefit holds in randomized Phase 2, mRNA neoantigen vaccines become a credible adjuvant SOC component for resectable PDAC — the first immunotherapy to do so. *Pre-embargo: Phase 2 readout timing not confirmed publicly; could land here, ESMO 2026, or 2027.*

### 4. KRAS-G12C(ON) RMC-6291 PDAC subset update

- **Sponsor:** Revolution Medicines.
- **What we'd watch:** Whether the G12C(ON) class (active-state binder) outperforms the G12C(OFF) class (sotorasib / adagrasib) in PDAC, where G12C(OFF) ORR has been modest.
- **Stakes:** Within the ~1.5% G12C-mut PDAC slice, this defines the second-generation standard. Mechanistically informs the broader "active-state vs inactive-state" KRAS-inhibitor design question. *Pre-embargo: data cut timing uncertain.*

### 5. Stromal / microenvironment major symposium

- **Lead:** Olive / Tuveson / Hingorani lineage + emerging spatial-omics groups.
- **What we'd watch:** Whether the iCAF / myCAF / apCAF resolution moves from descriptive to interventional — combination trial designs targeting one CAF compartment without the off-target stromal-collapse problem that sank prior stroma-targeting agents (hedgehog, hyaluronidase, FAK class).
- **Stakes:** Stroma has been "next year's story" at AACR Pancreatic for a decade. Whether 2026 is the cycle that produces a credible combination-trial nomination is the open question.
