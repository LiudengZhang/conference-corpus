# AACR Pancreatic Cancer 2026

**AACR Special Conference in Cancer Research: Pancreatic Cancer** — Hilton San Diego Bayfront, San Diego, CA · September 25 – 28, 2026. The AACR subspecialty meeting where the pancreatic-cancer field (basic + translational + clinical) convenes between AACR Annual and the late-year clinical-readout cycle.

> **Status:** Scaffold — meeting is **~4.5 months out** as of today (May 11, 2026). Structure is being laid down now so the abstract submission cycle (typical AACR Special Conference window: ~June deadline, late-breaker ~August), pre-meeting program release (August–September), and live-coverage build (Sep 25–28) can be staged. Talk titles, the late-breaking slate, and named-lecture assignments are **not yet public**; anything in this vault dated before its embargo lift is reconstructed from prior-year (Pancreatic 2024 Boston, Pancreatic 2025) AACR programs, AACR 2026 Annual pancreatic-track content, sponsor pipeline guidance, and analyst preview notes — flagged inline.

## Why this is in the vault

AACR Pancreatic is the **field-level convening venue** for PDAC research between the Annual Meeting (April) and the late-year clinical readouts (ESMO Oct, ASCO GI Jan). Two characteristics make it distinct from the broader [AACR 2026 Annual Meeting](../aacr-2026/index.md):

- **Single-disease depth.** The room is pancreatic-cancer-specialist-dominated — basic biologists (KRAS signaling, stroma, metabolism), translational scientists (PDX / organoid models, ctDNA / cfDNA biomarkers), and medical/surgical oncologists run cross-disciplinary discussions that the Annual Meeting's parallel-track format dilutes. Discussion sections after talks routinely run long; the small-room culture is a feature.
- **Hybrid format.** Unlike SABCS (clinical-readout-driven) or EuroBioC (per-tool methods talks), AACR Pancreatic mixes practice-changing clinical readouts (Phase 1b/2 KRAS-inhibitor data, mRNA vaccine durability updates), mechanistic talks (stromal biology, immune evasion, metabolic vulnerabilities), and platform / model-system talks (organoids, GEMMs, spatial atlases). The per-talk template in `talks/` is **hybrid** — each entry resolves to a *trial* anchor (clinical readout), *tool* anchor (model / platform / assay), or *study* anchor (mechanism paper / dataset).

The field is in an unusual moment. The KRAS-inhibitor wave that crossed the bar at AACR 2024–2025 (sotorasib / adagrasib for G12C; daraxonrasib pan-RAS at AACR 2025) is feeding the first wave of pancreatic-specific combination Phase 2 / 3 readouts. mRNA neoantigen vaccines (autogene cevumeran / BNT122) reported the first long-term recurrence-free survival signal at ASCO 2024, with follow-up readouts in the AACR Pancreatic → ESMO 2026 → ASCO GI 2027 cycle. Early-detection biomarker work (CA19-9 successors, microbiome signatures, MRI/CT AI) is moving from biomarker discovery to interventional trials. AACR Pancreatic 2026 is the venue where these three threads converge with the basic-biology audience.

Strong overlap with [AACR 2026 Annual](../aacr-2026/index.md) — the pancreatic-specific clinical-trials and KRAS sessions at Annual feed forward to expanded Phase 2 readouts at Pancreatic. Lighter overlap with [SITC 2026](../sitc-2026/index.md) (PDAC immunotherapy track), [ESMO 2026](../esmo-2026/index.md) (clinical Phase 3 pancreatic readouts), and [ASCO GI 2026 / 2027](../asco-gi-2026/index.md) (GI-specific Phase 3).

## Program shape

AACR Pancreatic 2026 runs **Friday September 25 through Monday September 28, 2026**, four days, Hilton San Diego Bayfront. Co-chairs:

- **Andrew J. Aguirre** (Dana-Farber Cancer Institute) — KRAS biology / functional genomics / PDX
- **Florencia McAllister** (MD Anderson) — microbiome, immune microenvironment, prevention/interception
- **Kenneth P. Olive** (Columbia / Herbert Irving) — stroma biology, GEMMs, drug delivery
- **Eileen O'Reilly** (Memorial Sloan Kettering) — clinical pancreatic-cancer trials lead
- **Diane M. Simeone** (UC San Diego) — early detection, cancer stem cells, host institution lead
- **Colin D. Weekes** (Mass General / Harvard) — translational clinical, KRAS-directed trials

The structure (per the 2022 / 2024 / 2025 recurring pattern):

- **Plenary / keynote lectures** — opening keynote (typically a field-defining figure: prior years have included Pasca di Magliano, Vonderheide, Tuveson, Hingorani); named lectures bookend mornings.
- **Major symposia (typically 6–8 across the meeting)** — thematic sessions of 4–6 invited + abstract-selected talks. Common 2024/2025 themes: tumor evolution, KRAS-directed therapy, the immunosuppressive microenvironment, metabolism, early detection / interception, models for translation, health disparities.
- **Mini-symposia / proffered-paper sessions** — abstract-selected short talks, typically 4–6 per session, distributed across thematic afternoons.
- **Poster sessions** — typically two large evening sessions; abstract floor ~400–600 posters.
- **Forum / interactive sessions** — career development, trainee networking, advocacy / patient-perspective sessions.
- **Press program** — informal; selected presentations get AACR press-release coverage when a sponsor topline is timed to the meeting.

**Expected tracks dominating the 2026 corpus** (based on prior-year load + 2026 pipeline state):

- **Pan-RAS and KRAS-selective inhibitors** — daraxonrasib (RMC-6236, Revolution Medicines) pan-RAS Phase 2/3 PDAC data; zoldonrasib (RMC-9805, KRAS-G12D selective) Phase 1/2; the broader RMC tri-complex portfolio (RMC-6291 G12C(ON), RMC-5552 mTORC1, RMC-4630 SHP2); next-generation Mirati/BMS G12D agents; Astellas/Boundless RAS portfolio; Amgen / Frontier Medicines KRAS-mut combinations.
- **mRNA neoantigen vaccines** — BNT122 / autogene cevumeran (BioNTech / Genentech) Phase 2 randomized data; the parallel Moderna / Merck mRNA-4157 individualized program if PDAC arm reads; T-cell receptor / TCR-T cellular therapies targeting shared neoantigens (KRAS-G12D / G12V class-I HLA-restricted).
- **Stromal biology and the immunosuppressive microenvironment** — CAF heterogeneity, TGF-β / IL-6 / CXCR4 axis, the LIF / JAK-STAT pathway, mechanical-tension biology, stromal reprogramming with focal adhesion / hyaluronidase / sonic-hedgehog-pathway updates. Strong AACR Pancreatic franchise per the Olive / Tuveson lineage.
- **PDX / organoid / GEMM models** — co-clinical trial designs, organoid-based drug screening platforms (Tuveson / Tiriac), patient-derived xenograft sensitivity portfolios, GEMM-derived combination screens for KRAS-resistance.
- **Early detection and biomarkers** — CA19-9 successors (multi-analyte serum panels, methylation-based assays from Guardant / GRAIL / Exact Sciences), microbiome-based detection (oral / stool signatures), MRI / CT AI for sub-2-cm lesion detection, intercept strategies for high-risk cohorts (BRCA / Lynch / familial / new-onset diabetes).
- **Metabolism, hypoxia, autophagy** — autophagy / lysosomal targeting (DC661 / hydroxychloroquine class), glutamine / pyrimidine metabolic vulnerabilities, MAPK-pathway-induced metabolic rewiring.
- **Immunotherapy beyond checkpoint blockade** — CD40 agonists (sotigalimab follow-up), CXCR4 antagonists, claudin 18.2 ADCs / CAR-T (zolbetuximab class extending into PDAC), bispecifics, TCR-T.
- **Health disparities, racial / ethnic differences in outcomes** — increasingly a programmed track at AACR Special Conferences; the disparities in pancreatic cancer incidence / mortality / trial access is a stated AACR priority.
- **Surgical / locoregional / radiation** — borderline-resectable management, neoadjuvant chemotherapy paradigms (mFOLFIRINOX vs gem/abrax), SBRT integration.
- **Health-system / clinical-trial design** — adaptive platform trials for PDAC (PRECISION-Panc, Precision Promise, AltCAP-style alternating regimens), real-world evidence.

## What's different from sibling vaults

- **AACR Annual ([../aacr-2026](../aacr-2026/index.md)) is the parent meeting.** AACR Annual's pancreatic-track sessions feed forward — many of the AACR Pancreatic 2026 talks will be by speakers who gave preview / mechanism talks at Annual in April.
- **SABCS / ASCO are clinical-readout-driven; AACR Pancreatic is hybrid.** Single-disease focus but with a strong basic-biology / translational backbone — the per-trial template from SABCS doesn't fully fit, and the per-tool template from EuroBioC doesn't fit. The hybrid `talks/` template is the resolution.
- **Smaller corpus than the Annual Meeting.** ~400–600 posters + ~80–120 oral / mini-symposium talks vs Annual's thousands. Deeper per-talk coverage is feasible.
- **September timing.** Lands between AACR Annual (April), ASCO (May/June), EHA (June), ESMO (October), and the late-year readout-heavy cycle (SABCS / ASH / ASCO GI). Good window for Phase 2 maturation data and mechanism-level translational reveals.
- **No AACR virtual-platform replay this year (per the 2024 / 2025 pattern).** Slide decks are not centrally archived; primary capture is the abstract supplement + AACR Meeting News + KOL trade-press recaps.
