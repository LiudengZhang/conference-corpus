# ECCB 2026

**25th European Conference on Computational Biology (ECCB)** — Geneva, Switzerland · August 31 – September 4, 2026. In-person. Organized by SIB Swiss Institute of Bioinformatics. Theme: *"Biodiversity, AI & Health: computational biology to address the challenges of our time."*

> **Status:** Scaffold — meeting upcoming (~16 weeks out as of May 11, 2026). Program shape is fixed (3 main-conference days + tutorials + Communities Day), all 4 keynote speakers announced, proceedings submissions closed (notifications mid-May 2026), highlight-talk submissions closed (notifications early-June 2026). The accepted-paper list is not yet public; tool-page bulk extraction triggers after the OUP *Bioinformatics* supplement posts (~late-July / mid-August 2026).

> **Why ECCB 2026 is a separate vault from ISMB 2026:** ISMB and ECCB co-locate in joint years (ISMB/ECCB 2025 Liverpool). 2026 is **not** a joint year — ISMB 2026 runs Jul 12–16 in Washington DC, and ECCB 2026 runs Aug 31–Sep 4 in Geneva as a standalone European event. Same proceedings journal (*Bioinformatics*, OUP), same broad COSI-style scope, partially overlapping speaker base, but distinct program committee (SIB-led), distinct accepted-paper list, distinct keynote slate. We keep them as sibling vaults rather than merging.

## Why this is in the vault

- **Methods-and-tools depth, European-flavored.** ECCB is the largest European computational-biology meeting (~1,000–2,000 attendees historical baseline; smaller than ISMB's ~2,000–3,000). The accepted-paper subset that produces tool pages is narrower than ISMB but with very high overlap with the EuroBioC2025 / GBCC2025 / Robinson-lab / Soneson / SIB / EMBL speaker base. Many tools we've already documented (footprintR, SpatialData, mia, Tidyomics, msqrob2, DESpace, sosta, signifinder, plyxp, atena, MultiAssayExperiment, OSTA) re-surface here with European-side updates or applied case studies.
- **Same peer-reviewed proceedings track as ISMB.** ECCB proceedings papers publish as an online-only open-access (CC-BY) supplementary issue of *Bioinformatics* (OUP), $715 USD APC, fully indexed in Medline/ISI, posted **before** the conference. So tool pages here can cite a published DOI before the meeting starts — same depth pattern as ISMB 2026, not the slide-deck-only pattern of EuroBioC / GBCC.
- **AACR axis fit.** Five named Scientific Areas map directly onto AACR-vault axes: **Genomics** (Reinert), **Transcriptomics** (Ohler), **Proteins/Structural Biology** (Bahar), **Systems Biology** (Valencia), and **Biodiversity/Sustainability** (Bärenfaller). The first four are core AACR-relevant; the fifth is the European-distinct dimension (env-bio + pathogen surveillance) that JPM and AACR don't cover. The ELIXIR Track adds a research-infrastructure layer (FAIR data, registries, GA4GH-adjacent standards) with no AACR equivalent.
- **Cross-vault people overlap, Europe-weighted.** The Robinson lab (Zurich), Soneson (FMI Basel), Crowell (CNAG Barcelona), Marconato (EMBL Heidelberg), Borman (Turku), Castelo (UPF Barcelona), Marini (IMBEI Mainz), Lahti (Turku), Beerenwinkel (ETH Zurich, ECCB 2026 Scientific Committee co-chair), Bitbol (EPFL, co-chair), Dessimoz (SIB, Programme Chair), Reinert (FU Berlin) → all are likely to surface here. The Geneva venue and SIB stewardship make this the highest-density European-side computational-biology meeting of 2026.
- **One Swiss/EU policy keynote.** Jeremy Farrar (WHO Assistant Director-General, ex-Wellcome) opens the meeting — atypical for a methods conference, signals the "AI & Health" theme arc. Worth a keynote treatment even though it isn't a tools talk.

## Program shape

ECCB 2026 runs Mon Aug 31 (opening + Farrar keynote) through Wed Sep 2 (closing keynote by Walczak, 16:00). The first three days are the main scientific program (3 paper/highlight sessions per day in parallel with 3 special-track sessions per day). Thu Sep 3 is tutorials/workshops + ISCB Student Council Symposium. Fri Sep 4 is Communities Day — SIB Community-led satellites (biodiversity, pathogen surveillance, infrastructures).

**Counts:**

- **4 Distinguished Keynotes** spread across Mon–Wed (Farrar opens Mon; Malaspinas + Quake on Tue; Walczak closes Wed)
- **9 main scientific sessions** over 3 days (Proceedings + Highlight talks)
- **9 special-track sessions** over 3 days (ELIXIR ×4, Industry ×1, Focus ×4)
- **5 Scientific Areas**: Genomics, Transcriptomics, Proteins/Structural Biology, Systems Biology, Biodiversity/Sustainability
- **Poster sessions** Tue + Wed (count TBD; Career Workshop runs alongside Wed poster session)
- **Public lecture** Mon afternoon (in French; outreach component) — atypical for methods meetings, reflects Geneva/SIB civic profile
- **Tutorials/Workshops + ISCB SCS** Thu Sep 3
- **Communities Day** Fri Sep 4 — independent satellites

**Opening ceremony note:** the President of the Swiss Confederation (Guy Parmelin) gives the opening address Mon Aug 31, 10:00. Worth noting in the day-1 digest as a policy / Swiss-Bio context signal — same role as Galatea Bio's Bustamante closing keynote at ISMB 2026, but on the European policy side.

## What's different from ISMB / EuroBioC / GBCC / RECOMB

- **Scale.** ~1,000–2,000 attendees (between EuroBioC's ~250 and ISMB's ~3,000). Tool-page extraction load is intermediate — expected ~30–60 entries after the bulk pass (vs. ISMB's ~60–120 and RECOMB's ~50–60).
- **Same proceedings journal as ISMB.** *Bioinformatics* (OUP) CC-BY supplement, ~$700 APC, posted pre-meeting. Tool entries can cite a real DOI before the talk happens — same depth pattern as ISMB, not RECOMB's per-paper partnering-journal split.
- **ELIXIR Track is the distinct dimension.** ISMB has BOSC (open-source / Bioconductor / Galaxy / scverse). RECOMB has no analogue. ECCB has the **ELIXIR Track** — a 4-session stream focused on European research infrastructures (registries, FAIR data, training, communities). This is where ELIXIR-Bio.tools, BioContainers, Galaxy Europe, EuropePMC, Ensembl, UniProt, IntAct, etc. typically surface. Tool entries from the ELIXIR Track are **infrastructure**, not algorithms; the per-tool template's `Status` field needs an `infra / registry` value.
- **Biodiversity/Sustainability area is unique.** None of ISMB / RECOMB / EuroBioC / GBCC has a dedicated biodiversity track. ECCB does (Bärenfaller, area chair), and Communities Day Fri Sep 4 includes a biodiversity satellite. Low AACR axis fit for individual tool pages, but high relevance for the cross-vault "pathogen + environmental + One Health" thread that connects to ISMB Microbiome COSI and JPM 2026 commercial-genomics framing.
- **Communities Day adds satellite events the main programs don't have.** Closest analogue is RECOMB's satellite workshops (May 24–25 Thessaloniki) — but RECOMB satellites are *before* the main program, ECCB Communities Day is *after*. Tool/community pages from Communities Day will need to be wired into the main `sessions/` index with a clear `Day 5 / Communities Day` label.
- **Single-track on Mon–Wed for plenary keynotes; parallel for sci sessions.** Mon–Wed has plenary keynote slots but 3 parallel sci + 3 parallel special-track sessions per day. Less of a parallel-track triage problem than ISMB's 22-COSI grid; comparable to EuroBioC's 2-room shape but at larger scale.
