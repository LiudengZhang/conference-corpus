# HyenaDNA — Nguyen et al. (Stanford Hazy Research + Harvard + Mila)

**One-line description** — A decoder-only causal genomic LM built on **Hyena implicit-convolution operators** (replacing self-attention with sub-quadratic long convolutions) that handles **context lengths up to 1 million single-nucleotide tokens** — a 500× context jump over Transformer-based DNA models, with the first demonstration of in-context learning in genomics.

## What it does

HyenaDNA is a decoder-only causal language model for DNA that abandons the transformer's quadratic-attention bottleneck in favour of **Hyena operators** — a stack of implicit long convolutions parameterised by small MLPs, combined with data-controlled gating. The headline result is operating on **1,000,000 single-nucleotide tokens** in a single forward pass, with full global context at every layer, at ~160× the throughput of a Transformer at matched length. Outputs are next-token logits for autoregressive sequence modelling and fixed-size pooled embeddings for downstream classification. It was the **first genomic FM to demonstrate in-context learning** (soft-prompt tuning, k-shot pattern induction) — the methodological seed that later motivated Evo and the JHU "genomic ICL" workshop paper at ICLR 2026.

## How it works

**Architecture.** Decoder-only causal sequence model. Each layer is a **Hyena operator**: an implicit convolution where the kernel is generated by a small feedforward network conditioned on positional features (not stored as fixed weights). The operator combines (1) data-controlled gating (multiplicative interactions between input projections, similar to GLU), (2) an implicit long convolution computed in O(N log N) via FFT, and (3) shortcut connections. This replaces self-attention entirely — **no attention layers in the model** — and is the key to sub-quadratic scaling.

**Tokenisation.** **Single-nucleotide tokens** — vocabulary size 12 (A, C, G, T, N, plus special tokens for padding, masking, BOS, EOS, and reverse-complement markers). This is the strongest possible resolution for SNV-effect prediction: no information is lost at any base.

**Pretraining corpus.** Human reference genome **HG38**, approximately **3.2 billion base pairs** (one human genome). This is dramatically less data than NT (174 Gb multispecies) or DNABERT-2 (32 Gb multispecies) — the parameter / data efficiency is part of the methodological argument.

**Pretraining objective.** Causal next-token prediction (standard autoregressive LM). No masking, no bidirectional context. This unlocks generation and in-context learning that NT/DNABERT-2 cannot natively support.

**Context length variants.** Six checkpoints released, scaling depth (2–8 layers), width (128–256 hidden dim), and pretraining sequence length:
- `tiny-1k-seqlen` — 1,024-token context, smallest (~0.4M params)
- `tiny-1k-seqlen-d256` — 1,024 tokens, wider hidden dim
- `small-32k-seqlen` — 32,768-token context
- `medium-160k-seqlen` — 163,840 tokens
- `medium-450k-seqlen` — ~450k tokens
- `large-1m-seqlen` — **1,000,000-token context** — the headline variant; trained on A100-80GB GPUs

Parameter counts across the family span roughly 0.4M – 6.6M (the largest configuration is still under 7M params — orders of magnitude smaller than NT and Evo). The dnaGrinder follow-up paper documents the exact configuration table.

**Pretraining compute.** Headline efficiency claim: **1500× fewer parameters and 3200× less pretraining data than NT-2.5B** (1.6M params vs 2.5B; 1 human ref genome vs 3,202 in 1000G). Training throughput is up to 160× faster than a same-context Transformer for long sequences. Total pretraining wall-clock for `large-1m-seqlen` is single-digit A100-days (per repo training logs).

**Downstream tasks.** Evaluated on (1) the **18-task Nucleotide Transformer benchmark** suite (histone marks, regulatory annotation), (2) the **GenomicBenchmarks** suite of 8 short-context classification tasks, and (3) a novel **species classification at very long context** task that requires >100 kb — designed to stress-test long-range modelling.

## Headline benchmarks

- **GenomicBenchmarks (8 tasks):** HyenaDNA exceeds prior SOTA on **7 of 8 datasets**, average **+10 absolute accuracy points** over the previous best (a DNABERT-1 / CNN baseline).
- **Nucleotide Transformer 18-task suite:** HyenaDNA-32kb reaches SOTA on **12 of 18 tasks** — competitive with NT-500M but loses to NT-2.5B-multispecies on most regulatory tasks where multispecies-pretraining diversity matters more than long context. This is the canonical "single human genome, 1 Mb context" vs "850 species, 6 kb context" trade-off datapoint.
- **Species classification at 100 kb – 1 Mb:** HyenaDNA-large-1m succeeds where Transformer baselines fail to even fit the input — a "context-as-capability" demonstration rather than a head-to-head comparison.
- **In-context learning (soft-prompt tuning):** First demonstration of k-shot pattern induction in genomic LMs — a small soft-prompt trained on as few as 16 examples generalises to held-out classification — the methodological seed that motivated the JHU Sci4DL paper on ICL in Evo2 at ICLR 2026 (Breslow et al.).
- **vs DNABERT-2:** DNABERT-2 wins on short-range classification (promoter, splice-site, TFBS — GUE benchmark). HyenaDNA wins on tasks that genuinely require >10 kb. Confirms the two-axis design space (tokenisation × context).
- **vs AlphaGenome:** HyenaDNA is a *generative* causal LM; AlphaGenome is a *supervised* sequence-to-track regressor. They are not direct competitors on variant-effect prediction — AlphaGenome dominates regulatory-variant scoring, but HyenaDNA can sample novel sequences and serve as an ICL backbone, which AlphaGenome cannot.

## Why it matters

HyenaDNA established **three** results that shaped the 2024–2026 genomic-FM landscape:

1. **Sub-quadratic operators are sufficient for DNA.** Hyena (and later Mamba / SSM derivatives like Caduceus) replaced attention as the default long-context primitive in DNA. Evo and Evo2 (Arc Institute) directly inherit the Hyena-style implicit-convolution backbone — Evo2's 1 Mb context at 40B params is HyenaDNA's recipe at scale.
2. **Single-nucleotide tokenisation works.** Before HyenaDNA, the field assumed k-mer / BPE compression was necessary; HyenaDNA proved a vocabulary of 12 is enough if the operator is the right shape. AlphaGenome's per-base decoder, Evo's single-base tokenisation, and the NTv3 single-base variant all trace back to this argument.
3. **In-context learning is possible in DNA models.** This is the methodological hinge that converts genomic FMs from "fine-tuning backbones" into "queryable knowledge bases" — and is what the JHU group is now systematically probing in Evo2 at ICLR 2026.

## Known limitations

- **Single-species pretraining.** HG38-only pretraining underperforms multi-species models (NT-2.5B-multispecies) on regulatory tasks where evolutionary conservation is the signal. The Caduceus follow-up (NeurIPS 2024) and Evo (Arc Institute 2024) both fix this by adding multi-species data.
- **No bidirectional context.** Causal LM means each token sees only its left context. For variant-effect scoring this requires either dual-direction inference (forward + reverse-complement) or post-hoc symmetrisation; HyenaDNA does ship reverse-complement augmentation but it's not as principled as bidirectional MLM (NT / DNABERT-2) or the U-Net-with-transformer-bottleneck of AlphaGenome.
- **Small parameter count.** At ~6.6M parameters maximum, HyenaDNA has limited capacity for storing complex regulatory grammar. Evo (7B) and Evo2 (40B) scale the same Hyena recipe ~10,000× larger and dominate HyenaDNA on most downstream tasks accordingly.
- **Hyena kernel is data-dependent in a subtle way.** The implicit-convolution kernels are parameterised by positional features and can struggle with highly periodic / repetitive sequences (centromeres, satellite DNA) — a known failure mode of long convolutions.
- **In-context learning is unstable at small scale.** The ICL demonstration in the paper uses soft-prompt tuning rather than zero-shot prompting; the JHU ICLR 2026 work shows that **organically emergent** ICL only appears at Evo2-scale (40B params).
- **Memory for 1M-token inference.** Even with FlashFFT-style optimisations, forward-pass memory at 1 Mb context requires A100-40GB minimum.
