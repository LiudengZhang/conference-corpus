# ASHG 2026

**76th American Society of Human Genetics Annual Meeting** — Palais des Congrès de Montréal, Montréal, Québec, Canada · October 20 – 24, 2026. ~7,500 attendees from 75 countries — the largest human-genetics and genomics meeting of the year.

> **Status:** Scaffold — meeting is **~5 months out** as of today (May 11, 2026). The structure lands now so we can move quickly on the abstract submission window (closes **May 18, 2026**), the program-at-a-glance release (typically early-September), and the live-coverage build during the meeting itself. Anything in this vault dated before the abstract supplement / program release is built from ASHG meeting-site previews, prior-year program shape (ASHG 2025 Boston), and partner-lab press from JPM 2026 / AACR 2026 — flagged inline.

## Why this is in the vault

ASHG is the **methods-and-biology source for the cancer-genomics corpus**. Same investigators as AACR's population-genetics, hereditary-cancer, and bioinformatics sessions — one venue earlier, with a much wider denominator (Mendelian disease, prenatal, population genetics, ethics). The overlap with the AACR / ASCO oncology cycle is asymmetric but load-bearing:

1. **Hereditary cancer panels and germline-somatic integration.** ASHG is where the panel-design talks, ClinVar curation updates, and variant-classification debates live. The same investigators show up at AACR with somatic-overlay analyses six months earlier.
2. **Polygenic risk scores in oncology.** PRS methodology is an ASHG specialty (UK Biobank, All of Us, MVP, FinnGen, GBMI). Cancer-PRS talks at ASHG seed the clinical-utility readouts that arrive at ASCO 12–18 months later.
3. **Large-cohort sequencing readouts.** All of Us, UK Biobank WGS, gnomAD updates, GREGoR, GenomeAsia, H3Africa — all premiere at ASHG. Cancer-relevant subsets (germline pan-cancer, somatic mosaicism, clonal hematopoiesis) anchor downstream AACR / ASH talks.
4. **Methods development.** GWAS pipelines, rare-variant burden tests, fine-mapping, ancestry inference, single-cell genetics — many of the tools that show up in AACR cancer-genomics posters debut as ASHG methods talks.

**Hybrid template (and the design decision behind this vault):** ASHG is **neither pure-clinical (ASCO) nor pure-tools (EuroBioC)** — most program slots are "investigator-talk anchored on a study + method + cohort," so the unit of analysis is **the talk**, not the trial and not the tool. The closest sibling shape in the vault is [SITC 2026](../sitc-2026/index.md), which runs trials + tools side-by-side. For ASHG we collapse to a single `talks/` directory with a schema that accommodates both flavors (tool-anchored / clinical-study-anchored / methods-anchored), with the cancer-genomics subset prioritized.

## Program shape (per ASHG 2025 Boston + early ASHG 2026 announcements)

ASHG 2026 runs **Tuesday Oct 20 – Saturday Oct 24**, five days, Palais des Congrès de Montréal. Expected structure (per ASHG 2025 shape; ASHG 2026 program-at-a-glance not yet public):

- **Tue Oct 20: Pre-Meeting Workshops + Opening Plenary.** Pre-meeting workshops (~8–12 parallel; trainee primers, methods deep-dives, consortium meetings). Opening plenary + Presidential Address evening.
- **Wed Oct 21 – Sat Oct 24: Annual Meeting** proper. Plenary + ~6 parallel platform sessions + poster halls + invited symposia.
- **Presidential Symposium (theme: "Communicating Science in an Uncertain World: Evidence, Storytelling, and Public Trust").** Susan A. Slaugenhaupt (MGH / Harvard) moderating. *expected, program not yet public.*
- **Award Lectures (load-bearing slots, all *expected, recipients not yet announced*):**
  - **William Allan Award** — society's highest honor; career-achievement in human genetics. Award lecture is a Plenary slot.
  - **Curt Stern Award** — mid-career investigator; landmark contribution. Award lecture is a Plenary slot.
  - **Victor A. McKusick Leadership Award** — leadership / mentorship recognition.
  - **Mendel Lecture** — historically the international-genetics keynote (often a European or Asian society partner).
  - **Advocacy Award**, **Early-Career Award**, **Trainee Awards** — secondary slots; Trainee Research Excellence Award winners present in plenary slots.
- **Abstract scale:** ~3,000+ accepted abstracts (per ASHG 2025); ~600 platform / featured-plenary slots; ~2,500+ posters across three sessions.
- **Tracks dominating the corpus** (per ASHG 2025 shape; titles may shift):
  - **Mendelian disease & rare disease** — variant curation, GREGoR / UDN updates, gene discovery, diagnostic-yield in WGS-first programs.
  - **Population genetics & evolution** — ancestry inference, demographic history, archaic admixture, selection scans, gnomAD updates.
  - **Statistical genetics / GWAS / PRS** — biobank-scale association, fine-mapping, PRS methodology, cross-ancestry transferability.
  - **Cancer genetics** *(cancer-genomics overlap track)* — hereditary cancer panels, germline-somatic integration, somatic mosaicism, clonal hematopoiesis, cancer-PRS.
  - **Prenatal / reproductive genetics** — NIPT updates, expanded carrier screening, fetal sequencing.
  - **Clinical genetics & diagnostics** — RNA-seq for variant interpretation, long-read clinical sequencing, structural variant calling.
  - **Bioinformatics / computational methods** — variant calling, imputation, single-cell genetics, machine-learning variant effect predictors, foundation models for genomics.
  - **Ethics, Legal & Social Implications (ELSI)** — return of results, ancestry / identity, genetic privacy, AI ethics in genomics.
  - **Functional genomics** — CRISPR screens, MPRAs, base-editor saturation mutagenesis, perturb-seq.
  - **Education, Training, Workforce** — and a strong public-policy / advocacy thread (ASHG has a federal advocacy arm).

## What's different from sibling meetings

- **Wider denominator than AACR.** AACR is cancer-only; ASHG is all of human genetics with cancer as one of ~10 tracks. The cancer-genomics subset of ASHG is small in absolute terms but high-value because the methods land here first.
- **Methods-paper density.** Closer in flavor to ISMB / RECOMB than to AACR — many ASHG plenary talks are accompanied by a Nature Genetics / AJHG / Cell paper landing the same week. The talks-paper coupling is unusually tight.
- **Cohort-and-consortium-heavy.** All of Us, UK Biobank, gnomAD, GREGoR, MVP, FinnGen, H3Africa, GenomeAsia, GBMI updates anchor the program. Cohort talks at ASHG read like infrastructure announcements at a tools meeting.
- **No drug names.** Unlike ASCO, ASCO Plenary, ASH, ESMO — ASHG talks are about variants, cohorts, methods, and biology. Drug-development cross-links are downstream (cancer-PRS → trial-stratification at ASCO; hereditary-cancer panel → SERENA-style trial design).
- **Open-access disposition.** AJHG and HGGA are both Cell Press, both increasingly open-access; AoU and UK Biobank are public-dashboard. Citability is high; paywalls are low.
- **Trainee-heavy.** ASHG runs the largest trainee-award and travel-support program of any society in genomics. Trainee Research Excellence Award presenters often anchor the most-cited talks of the meeting.

## Cross-vault links

- **[AACR 2026](../aacr-2026/index.md)** — cancer-biology / mechanism side. Hereditary-cancer panels, germline-somatic integration, and cancer-PRS investigators overlap. Many ASHG cancer-genetics PIs have AACR sessions in April.
- **[ASCO 2026](../asco-2026/index.md)** — clinical translation. Cancer-PRS for trial stratification, germline-informed CDK4/6 / PARP-i selection — methods that mature at ASHG arrive at ASCO with patient outcomes.
- **[ASH 2026](../ash-2026/index.md)** — clonal hematopoiesis bridge. CHIP / CCUS / MN-predisposition is a shared topic; ASHG runs the population-genetics framing, ASH runs the clinical sequelae.
- **[ISMB 2026](../ismb-2026/index.md) / [RECOMB 2026](../recomb-2026/index.md)** — methods overlap (variant calling, deep-learning variant effect predictors, foundation models for genomics). Some methods papers have a talk at all three meetings in a single year.
- **[EuroBioC 2025 tools](../eurobioc-2025/tools/index.md)** — Bioconductor packages used in ASHG analyses (statistical-genetics tooling, multi-omics, single-cell).
- **[AGBT 2026](../agbt-2026/index.md)** — platform-launch overlap. Long-read clinical sequencing, single-cell-spatial, and methylome platforms that debut at AGBT in February show up applied at ASHG in October.
- **[Conferences timeline](../../timeline.md)** — ASHG's position in the year's genomics cycle (AGBT Feb → AACR Apr → ASCO May/Jun → ISMB Jul → ESMO/ASHG Oct → SITC Nov → ASH Dec → JPM Jan).
