# G4X Spatial Sequencer — Singular Genomics

**Singular's high-throughput spatial sequencer, officially released the week of AGBT and positioned head-to-head with Xenium / MERSCOPE / CosMx.**

## At a glance

- **Vendor / launch date / availability:** Singular Genomics; officially released the week of AGBT 2026 (Marco Island, Feb 22–25) *per [GEN AGBT 2026 recap, Feb 2026](https://www.genengnews.com/topics/omics/agbt-2026-recap-ngs-big-bets-and-spatials-rising-momentum/)*.
- **Chemistry / modality:** Spatial multiomics sequencer (in-situ readout on tissue sections).
- **Throughput (PR):** **128 samples per run across 40 cm² of tissue area** *per [GEN AGBT 2026 recap, Feb 2026](https://www.genengnews.com/topics/omics/agbt-2026-recap-ngs-big-bets-and-spatials-rising-momentum/)* **(vendor PR — no independent benchmark)**.
- **Workflow:** 5-day end-to-end workflow *per [GEN AGBT 2026 recap, Feb 2026](https://www.genengnews.com/topics/omics/agbt-2026-recap-ngs-big-bets-and-spatials-rising-momentum/)* **(vendor PR — no independent benchmark)**.
- **Reagent cost:** ~$100s/sample reagent cost *per [GEN AGBT 2026 recap, Feb 2026](https://www.genengnews.com/topics/omics/agbt-2026-recap-ngs-big-bets-and-spatials-rising-momentum/)* **(vendor PR — no independent benchmark)**.

## What's new vs prior generation

G4X is Singular's first dedicated spatial product, extending the G4 short-read sequencer chassis into in-situ tissue readout. The 128-samples-per-run footprint and 40 cm² tissue capacity are the highest sample-multiplexing numbers claimed by any AGBT 2026 spatial vendor; the 5-day workflow is positioned as competitive with Xenium's multi-day cycle.

## Reception / competitive context

Trade coverage placed G4X as the surprise spatial entrant of AGBT 2026, with the per-sample cost pitch directly threatening Xenium's reagent economics. [GenomeWeb's spatial roundup (Feb 2026)](https://www.genomeweb.com/business-news/agbt-showcases-diversity-spatial-genomics-technologies) frames G4X alongside Bruker CellScape, Vizgen MERSCOPE Ultra, and Stellaromics Pyxa as the cohort of platforms now competing for shelf space against the incumbent Xenium / MERSCOPE / CosMx trio.
