# MERSCOPE Ultra / MERFISH 2.0 — Vizgen

**Vizgen's MERSCOPE Ultra refresh extends predesigned panel coverage and adds organoid support, keeping MERFISH in the in-situ multiomics race against Xenium, CosMx, and G4X.**

## At a glance

- **Vendor / launch date / availability:** Vizgen; roadmap presented at AGBT 2026 (Marco Island, Feb 22–25); commercial availability **TBD** *per [GEN AGBT 2026 recap, Feb–Mar 2026](https://www.genengnews.com/topics/omics/agbt-2026-recap-ngs-big-bets-and-spatials-rising-momentum/)*.
- **Chemistry / modality:** MERFISH 2.0 — combinatorial error-robust single-molecule FISH; cyclic imaging on the MERSCOPE Ultra instrument.
- **Panel breadth (PR):** Expanded predesigned panels (specific gene-count tiers **TBD** — vendor PR did not enumerate plex levels at AGBT) *per [GEN AGBT recap](https://www.genengnews.com/topics/omics/agbt-2026-recap-ngs-big-bets-and-spatials-rising-momentum/)* **(vendor PR — no independent benchmark)**.
- **Sample type:** FFPE, fresh-frozen, plus **new organoid support** *per [GEN AGBT recap](https://www.genengnews.com/topics/omics/agbt-2026-recap-ngs-big-bets-and-spatials-rising-momentum/)* **(vendor PR — no independent benchmark)**.
- **Proteomics roadmap:** MERSCOPE-protein co-detection flagged on the Vizgen roadmap; specifics **TBD**.
- **List price / cost-per-sample:** **TBD** — no public pricing at AGBT.

## What's new vs prior generation

MERSCOPE Ultra is positioned as the next-generation instrument refresh: expanded *predesigned* panels (reducing time-to-data for users who don't need a custom MERFISH library design), explicit organoid workflow support (a tissue type the original MERSCOPE could handle but not at fully validated reagent-kit level), and proteomic co-detection on the roadmap. The pitch is to keep MERFISH competitive on plex and image quality while removing the custom-panel-design tax that historically slowed MERSCOPE adoption versus the more turnkey Xenium / CosMx panel catalogs.

## Reception / competitive context

Vizgen entered AGBT 2026 as a defensive incumbent: MERFISH retains a plex and quantitative-image-quality narrative, but the platform has lost shelf-space momentum to Xenium and CosMx over 2024–2025. The Ultra / 2.0 refresh and the organoid-support angle are designed to keep MERSCOPE on the consideration set against [Xenium RNA+Protein](../launches/index.md), [CosMx mouse WT + 64-protein](bruker-cosmx-mouse-wt.md), and the newcomer [Singular Genomics G4X spatial sequencer](../launches/index.md). The [GenomeWeb spatial recap](https://www.genomeweb.com/business-news/agbt-showcases-diversity-spatial-genomics-technologies) lists Vizgen alongside Bruker and 10x as the established in-situ-imager trio that G4X and Stellaromics are now attacking.
