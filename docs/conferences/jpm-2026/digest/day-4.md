# Day 4 — Thursday Jan 15, 2026

The closing day of the 44th J.P. Morgan Healthcare Conference was, as expected, a wind-down — but with one significant late-week deal: **Boston Scientific's $14.5B acquisition of Penumbra**. Otherwise the day shifted to small- and micro-cap presenters, Chinese pharma, an interview with Regeneron's chief scientist George Yancopoulos, and end-of-week synthesis from the trade press. The mood reporters distilled at the close was one of "**pragmatic optimism**" and "**restraint**" rather than fireworks.

---

## 1. Boston Scientific — $14.5B acquisition of Penumbra

- Press release dated **Jan 15, 2026**.
- Adds Penumbra's **stroke / peripheral vascular / venous thromboembolism** thrombectomy portfolio to BSx's interventional cardiology and vascular franchises.
- Largest single deal of JPM week 2026; only true megadeal of the conference.

**Why it matters:** The exception that proves the rule — JPM26 was overwhelmingly licensing-driven; this one strategic medtech acquisition stood out against an otherwise megadeal-free week.

Source: [Boston Scientific press release](https://news.bostonscientific.com/2026-01-15-Boston-Scientific-announces-agreement-to-acquire-Penumbra,-Inc).

---

## 2. Clover Health — on track for first full-year GAAP profit

- CEO Andrew Toy presented Thursday at 11:15 a.m. ET.
- Clover said it is on track for its **first full-year GAAP profit in 2026**.
- **53% YoY membership growth** in the most recent annual enrollment period; **~153,000 members** entering the year, with **97% in the company's central PPO**.
- The PPO plan claims a **#1 national ranking** on core HEDIS quality measures.

**Why it matters:** Clover joins Alignment Healthcare in a **counter-narrative to MA giants pulling back**; signals smaller, tech-enabled MA plans may pick up share as larger players retreat.

Sources: [Modern Healthcare](https://www.modernhealthcare.com/providers/mh-jpm-2026-live-updated/); [Fierce Healthcare](https://www.fiercehealthcare.com/payers/jpm26-clover-health-poised-profitability-year).

## 3. Regeneron — Yancopoulos interview

- STAT sat down with Regeneron co-founder/chief scientist **George Yancopoulos** on Day 4. (Regeneron's formal JPM presentation was Monday.)
- Outspoken on the Trump administration's **"most-favored nations" drug-pricing policy** — explained why Regeneron was the only one of 17 companies receiving presidential pricing letters that had not yet struck a deal at that point. (Regeneron later signed a pricing deal in April 2026.)
- Voiced **skepticism about current AI applications in medicine**, calling for careful oversight.
- On gene therapy, he framed **scaling as the unsolved problem**.

**Why it matters:** Highest-profile scientific voice at Regeneron pushing back on **both AI hype and federal pricing pressure** on the conference's final day.

Sources: [STAT — Yancopoulos profile](https://www.statnews.com/2026/01/15/regeneron-george-yancopoulos-jpm-biotech/); [STAT — Day 4 top stories](https://www.statnews.com/2026/01/15/jpm-2026-day-4-top-stories-regeneron-fda-ipos-jnj/).

## 4. Day 4 small/mid/micro-cap presenters

- **Inventiva (MASH):** Phase 3 for **lanifibranor** is fully enrolled; Phase 2b F2–F3 data + safety profile cited as setting up 2026 readouts.
- **Precigen:** debuted as a commercial-stage company following the 2025 FDA approval of **PAPZIMEOS** (zopapogene imadenovec-drba) for adult recurrent respiratory papillomatosis; high coverage rates and rapid uptake.
- **Biocon:** outlined 2026 launches across biosimilars and GLP-1s; framed itself as moving from post-acquisition consolidation into an "accelerate" phase.
- **Oruka, Vicore:** clinical/commercial catalysts in dermatology, rare disease, and IPF.
- **Atea (HCV), Corbus (ADC + obesity), Molecular Partners (radiotherapeutics):** each presented as micro-cap update stories.
- **Rakuten Medical:** closed the day with a photoimmunotherapy platform update.

**Why it matters:** These are the catalyst stocks BD teams scout on the quiet final day; Evaluate framed Day 4 as "**BD signal day**" rather than headline day.

Source: [Evaluate Day Four](https://www.evaluate.com/blog/a-clearer-view-from-jpm-2026-day-four/).

## 5. Chinese pharma — Hengrui and Kelun-Biotech

- **Hengrui** reported 2025 sales through Q3 of **>$3.2B** and **21 NDA/BLA approvals**.
- Hengrui flagged a **$1.5B Hong Kong IPO** and a cumulative **~$16B in out-licensing deals** (including a partnership with GSK), alongside its Shanghai listing.

## End-of-conference recap themes

### Tone: restraint and execution, not fireworks

- Fierce Biotech ("**All the meetings, none of the megadeals**") and STAT's STATus Report (Alex Hogan) both used the words "restraint" and "fundamentals" to describe the week.
- C&EN: "**Despite few big announcements at JPM, biotech investors optimistic.**"

### Capital markets / M&A outlook

- pharmaphorum and Ballard Spahr: **no pharma megamergers**; many partnerships, smaller tech-tuck-in M&A, and a few IPO announcements. Pre-JPM **Lilly–Ventyx ($1B+)** acquisition was cited as the largest near-conference deal. (BSx–Penumbra **$14.5B medtech** was the only true megadeal during the week.)
- Pharmaceutical Technology: **~$8.3B in announced JPM-week deals**; emphasis on oncology (PD-1/VEGF bispecifics, ADCs), obesity, and neurodegeneration.
- **IPO forecast** (HSBC Innovation Banking's Jonathan Norris, cited by STAT/BioSpace): up to **~20 biotech IPOs** may price in 2026 if conditions hold; <12 priced in 2025 (lowest since at least 2018); most 2025 IPOs trade above offer, with **Sionna and Maze more than doubling**. **Aktis Oncology ($318M)**, **Eikon Therapeutics (~$381M, biggest biotech IPO since 2024)** and **Veradermics (~$256.3M, NYSE: MANE)** were the headline IPO/IPO-target stories.

### AI: from hype to ROI

- STATus Report (Alex Hogan): AI is "**table stakes**," exemplified by the Lilly–NVIDIA expanded partnership announced on Day 1.
- Advisory.com top-5 takeaways and Mondaq/Ballard Spahr both wrote that AI conversations shifted to **measurable ROI and compliance** rather than promise.
- Hogan Lovells flagged **AI compliance and BIOSECURE** as top device-supply-chain concerns.

### Therapeutic-area winners and losers

- **Winners:** oncology (ADCs, bispecifics, radiopharmaceuticals); obesity (oral GLP-1s, combinations — Pfizer's Bourla compared the cash-pay obesity opportunity to **Viagra** during the week); rare disease; neuroscience; immunology.
- **Headwinds: FDA dysfunction** was a recurring complaint — Sanofi singled out the Dec 24, 2025 Complete Response Letter on **tolebrutinib**, citing the FDA's skipped advisory committee step.

### Surprises / what was notably absent

- **Notably absent:** a JPM-week pharma megamerger. Fierce Biotech's "All the meetings, none of the megadeals" was the canonical framing.
- **Notably present:** China dominance (the "**Sputnik moment**" framing from BioPharma Dive on Day 1 carried through to Day 4 with Hengrui/Kelun).
