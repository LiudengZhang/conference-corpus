# Day 2 — Tuesday Jan 13, 2026

Day 2 was driven by **big-pharma strategy presentations** rather than blockbuster M&A, with AstraZeneca, Novo Nordisk, GSK, Bayer, Roche, Pfizer, Sanofi, Gilead and Sandoz on the main stage. Two notable deals broke during the day — **AstraZeneca's tuck-in of AI partner Modella** and **Novartis' $50M radioligand license** from China's Zonsen PepLib — alongside multiple mid-cap pipeline updates. The political/regulatory undercurrent was loud: former FDA oncology chief **Richard Pazdur** made his first public remarks since departing the agency, and CMS chief Mehmet Oz fielded hospital-exec questions at an off-site event.

---

## 1. AstraZeneca acquires Modella AI

- AZ buying Boston-based Modella AI; **financial terms undisclosed**.
- Builds on a multi-year research collaboration signed in July 2025; CFO Aradhana Sarin called the partnership a "test drive."
- Modella's **multi-modal foundation models** and AI agents will be integrated into AZ's oncology R&D for biomarker discovery, clinical-development acceleration, and data-driven decisioning.
- Described in trade-press coverage as the first acquisition of an AI firm by a big pharma.

**Why it matters:** Signals big pharma moving from AI partnerships to **outright ownership** of multimodal-AI platforms in oncology.

Sources: [Modella AI release](https://www.modella.ai/az-acquisition); [Pharmaceutical Technology](https://www.pharmaceutical-technology.com/news/jpm26-astrazeneca-bids-to-accelerate-oncology-rd-with-modella-ai-acquisition/); [Fierce Biotech tracker](https://www.fiercebiotech.com/biotech/fierce-biotechs-jpm26-tracker-day-2).

## 2. Novartis licenses peptide radioligand from Zonsen PepLib (China)

- **$50M cash upfront** to Zonsen PepLib Biotech for an undisclosed peptide-based **radioligand therapy asset**.
- Exclusive worldwide license; Novartis takes development and commercialization; PepLib eligible for undisclosed milestones plus tiered royalties.

**Why it matters:** Extends Novartis' leadership in radioligand therapy and continues the trend of big-pharma in-licensing from Chinese biotechs.

Sources: [Endpoints News](https://endpoints.news/novartis-licenses-radiopharma-asset-from-chinese-biotech-for-50m-upfront/); [BusinessWire](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260112577341/en/).

## 3. AstraZeneca strategy update — $80B by 2030 reaffirmed

- Revenue **up 11% through Q3 2025**; oncology contributing roughly half of growth.
- CFO Sarin called the **$80B revenue target** "very much within reach."
- Near-term launch focus: hypertension drug **baxdrostat**, breast-cancer candidate **camizestrant**, myasthenia gravis drug **gefurulimab**.
- Multiple Phase 3 readouts expected in 2026 across oncology, immunology, respiratory.

Source: [BioSpace](https://www.biospace.com/business/astrazenecas-path-to-80b-by-2030-paved-with-adcs-cell-therapies-near-term-product-launches).

---

## 4. Novo Nordisk — "mastering" DTP cash-pay; Wegovy pill ramping

- CEO Maziar Mike Doustdar acknowledged a "difficult 2025"; 2026 priorities = commercial execution, R&D pipeline, organizational focus.
- **Oral Wegovy** reached ~**3,100 patients since its Jan. 5 launch** — first oral GLP-1 for obesity in the U.S.
- Plans to advance higher-dose injectable Wegovy and "several" new obesity/diabetes assets into clinic in 2026.
- Novo took **~200 meetings during JPM week**. Pulling back from non-diabetes/obesity indications.
- CSO Martin Holst Lange (recently promoted) said Novo is craving "different flavors" of pipeline innovation.

Source: [Fierce Pharma JPM26 Day 2 tracker](https://www.fiercepharma.com/pharma/fierce-pharmas-jpm26-tracker-day-2).

## 5. Gilead — "unprecedented opportunities"; Kite anito-cel filing

- CEO Daniel O'Day said Gilead has "the most robust pipeline in the almost 40-year history of the company"; **up to 10 launches** in new/additional indications by end of 2027.
- Kite confirmed **FDA submission of anito-cel** (BCMA CAR-T for relapsed/refractory multiple myeloma, partnered with Arcellx) before year-end 2025; targeting differentiated one-time treatment in 2026 with **14–17 day turnaround**.

**Why it matters:** Anito-cel positions Kite to compete directly with J&J/Legend's Carvykti.

Source: [STAT News](https://www.statnews.com/2026/01/13/jpm-2026-day-2-top-stories-pazdur-travere-oz-pande/).

## 7. Bayer — Nubeqa and Kerendia anchor post-Xarelto plan

- 9M 2025 sales: **Nubeqa** (prostate cancer) ~**€1.7B (~$2B)**; **Kerendia** (kidney/heart failure) ~**€600M (~$700M)**.

## 8. Roche — three-pillar growth strategy

- CEO Teresa Graham: maximize current portfolio (**17 blockbusters generating $44.5B YTD** through Sept 2025), deliver 2026–27 launches, expand into Alzheimer's and obesity.
- **Giredestrant** (next-gen SERD) positioned to advance ER+ breast-cancer SOC via combos with PI3K and CDK4/6 inhibitors.

## 11. BioNTech — multiproduct oncology by 2030

- Ugur Sahin presented at 2:15pm PT Tuesday.
- 2026 = "catalyst-rich": **7 late-stage data readouts**, **15 Phase 3 trials** ongoing by year end.
- **Three-modality oncology strategy**: immunomodulators, ADCs, mRNA cancer immunotherapies.
- Key 2026 readouts: HER2 ADC (TPAM), Gotistobart, BNT113 (HPV+ head/neck), pumitamig (with BMS, June 2025 partnership worth up to $7.6B), personalized vaccine in high-risk colorectal.

Sources: [BioNTech IR](https://investors.biontech.de/news-releases/news-release-details/biontech-provides-strategic-business-update-and-outlines-2026/); [Pharmaceutical Technology](https://www.pharmaceutical-technology.com/news/jpm26-biontech-gears-up-for-multiproduct-oncology-status-by-2030/).

## Smaller items / notable mentions

- **Sandoz** (CEO Richard Saynor) framed itself as "the only pure-play generic and biosimilar player with global reach"; biosimilar pipeline of **27 assets targeting ~$200B** in originator sales; 400+ generics targeting ~$220B.
- **Acadia** — analysts modeling $834M Nuplazid and $621M Daybue revenue.
- **Agios** — touting Dec. 23 FDA approval of **Acvezmi** (thalassemia); $10B+ market opportunity by 2030; AG-181 (PKU) and AG-236 (polycythemia vera) flagged as ~$1B each.
- **Sarepta** — doubling down on Elevidys in ambulatory DMD; expanding field force; awaiting Endeavor cohort 8 readout late 2026.
- **Biogen** — CEO Viehbacher pitched "the new Biogen": diversified neuroscience, rare disease and revitalized pipeline.
- **Travere** — disclosed FDA extended its sNDA review for **Filspari/sparsentan in FSGS**; new PDUFA April 13, 2026.
- **Mid-cap pipeline progress** flagged by Evaluate: Crinetics, Cogent, Nuvalent, Edgewise.
- **Mirador Therapeutics** — closed **$250M Series B** (OrbiMed, Atlas Venture among investors).
- **Off-site policy events:** Richard Pazdur's first public remarks post-FDA departure at STAT@JPM (warning on politicization, voucher transparency); Mehmet Oz and Trump health officials briefed hospital execs on Medicaid and vaccines.

## Themes dominant on Day 2

- **AI moves from partnership to ownership** in pharma (AZ–Modella as exemplar).
- **China in-licensing pace remains high** (Novartis–PepLib, building on AbbVie–RemeGen on Day 1); China-sourced assets are now mainstream pipeline inputs, not bargain bins.
- **Big-pharma narrative is execution and late-stage delivery**, not transformative M&A — the rumored Merck/Revolution Medicines $32B deal stayed a rumor through Day 2.
- **Regulatory/political uncertainty** is a recurring sidebar: FDA voucher program scrutiny, Pazdur warnings, MFN pricing fallout for Sanofi, RFK Jr. vaccine rhetoric hitting Sanofi's Beyfortus.
- **Obesity remains the most-watched therapeutic area:** Novo's commercial recovery vs. Pfizer/Metsera entry vs. Roche teasing obesity entry.
