# Day 3 — Wednesday Jan 14, 2026

Day 3 unfolded as the conference's "final full day" with **no megadeal materializing** despite morning anticipation among bankers; instead the day skewed toward strategic narratives — AbbVie's post-Humira runway, Sanofi navigating U.S. vaccine policy turmoil, Novo Nordisk telegraphing M&A appetite under a new CEO, and a parade of mid-cap and rare-disease names making their case. Dominant Day 3 threads: **policy/politics, China innovation pace, obesity-pipeline jockeying, and rare disease as a pivotal-year story**.

> **Cross-day note:** The widely-cited Boston Scientific / Penumbra **$14.5B** acquisition was announced **Thursday Jan 15 (Day 4)**, not Day 3. See [Day 4](day-4.md).

---

## 1. AbbVie — "clear line of sight" to growth into the 2030s

- CFO Scott Reents in a Wednesday fireside chat said AbbVie has a "clear line of sight" extending into the 2030s, citing **8% 2025 revenue growth** and **19% growth in its growth platform**.
- Issued **2027 guidance of $31B combined for Skyrizi (~$20B) and Rinvoq (~$11B)**, now three years past the Humira LOE cliff.
- Came on the heels of AbbVie's Day-1 $5.6B RemeGen licensing deal (RC148, PD-1/VEGF bispecific); Day 3 framing emphasized organic engine, not M&A.

**Why it matters:** Re-anchors the post-Humira AbbVie story as **durable rather than transitional**.

Source: [Fierce Pharma](https://www.fiercepharma.com/pharma/fierce-pharmas-jpm26-tracker-day-3).

---

## 2. Sanofi — vaccine policy and RSV probe dominate the Wednesday press event

- R&D chief **Houman Ashrafian** and CEO **Paul Hudson** addressed renewed U.S. scrutiny of immunizations following CDC's **narrowed childhood RSV recommendation**, which directly touches **Beyfortus**.
- Sanofi flagged a recent surprise **FDA rejection (last week before JPM)** as an outstanding hurdle entering 2026.
- Hudson also discussed the company's late-2025 **"most favored nation" pricing arrangement** with the White House, which has temporarily skirted U.S. import-tariff threats.

**Why it matters:** Vaccines remain a **policy-risk asset class** for Merck, Pfizer, GSK, and Sanofi heading into 2026.

Sources: [Fierce Biotech](https://www.fiercebiotech.com/biotech/jpm26-after-important-year-sanofi-looks-ahead-remaining-federal-challenges); [Endpoints](https://endpoints.news/jpm26-day-3-at-the-jp-morgan-healthcare-conference/).

---

## 3. Novo Nordisk — M&A "with intention" under new CEO

- Novo's BD team logged **~200 meetings during JPM week**; CEO Mike Doustdar laid out 2026 priorities: rolling out semaglutide 7.2 mg, launching oral Wegovy, expanding U.S. cash-pay channels, and improving supply.
- Novo signaled appetite for novel obesity/diabetes mechanisms, tolerability/dosing improvements, **rare blood diseases**, and obesity-complication adjacencies.
- STAT framed Day 3 as the day Novo "has an eye on M&A" after losing the **Metsera bidding war to Pfizer**.

**Why it matters:** Confirms Novo is a **structural buyer in the obesity ecosystem** despite ceding the lead position to Lilly.

Sources: [STAT](https://www.statnews.com/2026/01/14/jpm-2026-day-3-top-stories-sanofi-bms-pfizer-biontech/); [BioSpace](https://www.biospace.com/business/deal-hungry-novo-moves-with-intention-to-put-metsera-in-the-rearview).

## 4. Amgen — MariTide Phase 2 (mid-week reprise)

- Amgen reiterated Phase 2 MariTide data: **up to 20% mean weight loss in obesity-only patients** and **up to 17% in obesity + T2D**.
- T2D arm: clinically meaningful HbA1c and body-weight reduction at 24 weeks on once-monthly dosing.
- Six global Phase 3 studies ongoing; a Phase 3 in T2D being prepared; safety profile consistent with GLP-1RA class.

**Why it matters:** Positions MariTide as a potential **first once-monthly T2D therapy** and Amgen's anchor in the obesity contest.

> *Coverage is bundled across mid-week; exact session day not pinned to Wednesday.*

Source: [Clinical Trials Arena](https://www.clinicaltrialsarena.com/news/jpm26-amgen-maritide-obesity-t2d-phase-ii/).

## 5. Regeneron — defends Dupixent strategy, M&A discipline

- CEO **Leonard Schleifer** defended Dupixent's role: now approved in **8 type-2 inflammation indications** with annualized global net sales **>$19B** (Q4 run-rate).
- Plans **$6B 2026 R&D spend**; flagged **2031 as the Dupixent LOE horizon**.
- Said large M&A often results in "value destruction" and that acquirers "in most cases, dramatically overpay."

**Why it matters:** Regeneron stakes out a **discipline posture** as peers chase deals through patent cliffs.

Sources: [Endpoints](https://endpoints.news/jpm26-day-3-at-the-jp-morgan-healthcare-conference/); [Pharmaceutical Technology](https://www.pharmaceutical-technology.com/news/jpm26-regeneron-will-not-overpay-in-ma-deals-amid-lead-asset-patent-expiries/).

## 6. Gilead — broadening virology beyond HIV/HCV

- CMO **Dietmar Berger** detailed plans to extend Gilead's antiviral footprint to **respiratory viruses and pandemic-potential pathogens** at a JPM-adjacent event.
- Builds on lenacapavir momentum; separate Fierce coverage that week framed Gilead as eyeing deals from a "**position of strength**."

**Why it matters:** Signals Gilead's intent to convert HIV cash flows into a **broader infectious-disease franchise**.

Source: [Fierce Biotech](https://www.fiercebiotech.com/biotech/jpm26-day-3-gileads-cmo-talks-virus-strategy).

---

## 7. Legend Biotech — operating profit guide for 2026, Carvykti scale-up

- Updated JPM presentation guides to **company-wide operating profit in 2026**; Carvykti franchise hit operating profit in Q3 2025.
- **10,000+ patients treated globally**; Raritan facility expansion targets **up to 10,000 patients/year** capacity; **279+ sites across 14 markets**.
- ~$1B cash and time deposits as of Sep 30, 2025.

**Why it matters:** Cell-therapy economics inflecting toward profitability in commercial CAR-T (with J&J).

Sources: [Legend Biotech IR](https://investors.legendbiotech.com/news-releases/news-release-details/legend-biotech-highlights-recent-business-updates-44th-annual-jp); [TipRanks](https://www.tipranks.com/news/company-announcements/legend-biotech-signals-2026-profitability-push-and-carvykti-expansion-in-updated-j-p-morgan-presentation).

## 8. Pfizer / BioNTech — COVID 2025-2026 formula immunogenicity readout

- Phase 3 cohort of **LP.8.1-adapted monovalent COVID-19 vaccine** met immunogenicity endpoints in adults 65+ and 18–64 with risk conditions; **≥4-fold rise** in LP.8.1-neutralizing titers at 14 days.

**Why it matters:** Supports continued strain-updated COVID franchise amid a more skeptical U.S. policy backdrop.

Source: [Pfizer press release](https://www.pfizer.com/news/press-release/press-release-detail/pfizer-and-biontech-announce-topline-data-demonstrating).

## Smaller items / notable mentions

- **Biotech CEO Sisterhood** second-annual Wednesday meetup drew "hundreds" — attendance roughly doubled from 2025.
- **Novartis CMO Shreeram Aradhye** on China speed: cautioned against simple speed comparisons, citing different ecosystem, trial-enrollment economics, and payer structure.
- **IPO market chatter:** as many as ~20 biotech IPOs could price in 2026 if windows hold (BioPharma Dive).
- **Rare-disease Wednesday spotlight:** Rocket Pharma CEO Gaurav Shah called 2026 a "year of execution"; REGENXBIO CEO Curran Simpson framed it as "really pivotal" pending Hunter syndrome (MPS II) approval; Agios highlighted PK-activator franchise growth.
- **Evaluate Day 3 readout:** notable presenters included AbbVie, UCB, Arcus, Disc Medicine, Immunome, Legend, 4DMT, Agios, Anavex, Neurogene, Tango.
- **Lonza** added contracts at the Vacaville, CA biologics site it bought from Roche for $1.2B in 2024.
- **No Wednesday megadeal** materialized; one attendee told Fierce some companies appear to be holding announcements for the **TD Cowen Health Care Conference in March**.

## Themes dominant on Day 3

- **Policy and politics threading through pharma narratives** — Sanofi's vaccine/tariff overhang, Pfizer/BioNTech's COVID franchise reframe, Moderna's break-even-by-2028 path.
- **Obesity competition** shifts from drug-data tournament to **commercial/distribution warfare** (Novo's DTP/oral-Wegovy push, Amgen's once-monthly differentiation, Pfizer-Metsera looming).
- **Rare disease and cell/gene therapy** as the "**year of execution**" story, aided by FDA's late-2025 plausible-mechanism pathway.
- **Capital-discipline rhetoric** (Regeneron) coexisting with **active BD pipelines** (Novo, Gilead) — few megadeals on Wednesday but strong tone of asset-centric, mid-size dealmaking.
