# Day 1 — Monday Jan 12, 2026

**Source:** synthesized from public coverage (see [Sources](#sources)). Unlike the Nextflow vault, this digest has no primary transcripts — JPM is invite-only and most content is reconstructed from trade-press reporting and company press releases.

The 44th J.P. Morgan Healthcare Conference opened in San Francisco with an unusually quiet news flow by historical standards: no megadeal M&A and no surprise blockbuster acquisitions. Day 1 was anchored by a **$1B Lilly–NVIDIA AI alliance**, a **$5.6B AbbVie/RemeGen PD-1×VEGF licensing deal**, and a **~$1.7B Novartis/SciNeuro Alzheimer's pact** — alongside a heavy slate of fireside chats from BMS, Pfizer, J&J, Regeneron, Biogen, Moderna, Alnylam, argenx, BridgeBio, Sarepta, Summit, and CRISPR Therapeutics. Multiple observers (Takeda R&D chief Andy Plump; investor Brad Loncar) flagged the slow Monday cadence as a contrast with prior years.

---

## 1. NVIDIA + Eli Lilly — $1B AI Co-Innovation Lab

- Up to **$1B over 5 years**, jointly funded, to build an AI Co-Innovation Lab in the SF Bay Area co-locating Lilly biology/scientific staff with NVIDIA AI engineers.
- Built on **NVIDIA BioNeMo** platform and **NVIDIA Vera Rubin** architecture; aim is closed-loop drug discovery plus AI applications across manufacturing and commercial.
- Kimberly Powell (NVIDIA VP, Healthcare) led the press briefing.

**Why it matters:** Largest pharma–AI infrastructure commitment announced at JPM26 Day 1; sets the tone for the AI/bio convergence theme.

Sources: [Lilly press release](https://investor.lilly.com/news-releases/news-release-details/nvidia-and-lilly-announce-co-innovation-ai-lab-reinvent-drug); [Clinical Trials Arena](https://www.clinicaltrialsarena.com/news/jpm26-eli-lilly-and-nvidia-deepen-ties-with-1bn-ai-partnership/).

## 2. AbbVie + RemeGen — $5.6B PD-1×VEGF bispecific licensing

- **$650M upfront, up to $4.95B in milestones**, plus tiered double-digit royalties; ex-Greater China rights to **RC148**, a PD-1/VEGF bispecific antibody.
- Therapeutic area: advanced solid tumors (NSCLC, CRC focus); Phase 2 asset.
- Strategic rationale: combination potential with AbbVie ADCs including telisotuzumab adizutecan (Temab-A).

**Why it matters:** Largest deal announced on Day 1; another China-origin asset crosses to Big Pharma; intensifies the PD-1×VEGF bispecific race (vs. Summit's ivonescimab).

Sources: [AbbVie press release](https://news.abbvie.com/2026-01-12-AbbVie-and-RemeGen-Announce-Exclusive-Licensing-Agreement-to-Develop-A-Novel-Bispecific-Antibody-for-Advanced-Solid-Tumors); [Fierce Biotech](https://www.fiercebiotech.com/biotech/abbvie-pens-56b-pact-remegen-join-pd1xvegf-bispecific-battle).

---

## 3. Novartis + SciNeuro — ~$1.7B Alzheimer's antibody pact

- **$165M upfront**, up to **~$1.5B** in development/regulatory/sales milestones plus royalties. Worldwide license.
- Asset: de novo amyloid-beta antibody candidates with SciNeuro's proprietary **brain-shuttle delivery technology** designed to improve BBB penetration.
- Therapeutic area: Alzheimer's disease; expected close 1H 2026.

**Why it matters:** Differentiated delivery tech vs. approved anti-amyloid antibodies; positions Novartis in an active neuro-licensing market.

Sources: [SciNeuro/PRNewswire](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/scineuro-and-novartis-enter-into-a-licensing-and-collaboration-agreement-for-next-generation-therapeutics-to-treat-alzheimers-disease-302658400.html); [Fierce Biotech](https://www.fiercebiotech.com/clinical-data/novartis-pays-scineuro-165m-ticket-another-shuttle-brain).

## 4. Bristol Myers Squibb — CEO showcases blockbuster pipeline

- CEO Chris Boerner highlighted **six multi-billion-dollar candidates**: Cobenfy (Alzheimer's psychosis), milvexian (FXIa, J&J-partnered, AFib/stroke), admilparant (IPF), pumitamig (PD-L1×VEGF, BioNTech-partnered), and CELMoDs iberdomide & mezigdomide (multiple myeloma).
- Pivotal 2026 readouts: three Adept trials (Cobenfy in Alzheimer's psychosis), milvexian, admilparant, both CELMoDs.
- Targeting **>10 new launches by 2030**; BD remains a top capital-allocation priority.

**Why it matters:** Investor narrative for BMS hinges on pipeline diversification past LOEs.

Sources: [Fierce Pharma JPM26 Day 1 tracker](https://www.fiercepharma.com/pharma/fierce-pharmas-jpm26-tracker-day-1); [Endpoints Q&A with Boerner](https://endpoints.news/jpm26-qa-with-bristol-myers-squibb-ceo-chris-boerner-on-2026-outlook/).

## 5. Pfizer — obesity strategy on the back of $10B Metsera

- CEO Albert Bourla projected the **obesity market reaching $150B by 2030**; compared expected demand to the original Viagra rollout.
- Metsera acquisition (announced Sept 2025, closed Nov 2025) anchors a portfolio built around monthly injectables and oral combos, including **PF-3944/MET-097i** (ultra-long-acting GLP-1 in Ph2b).
- Plans **up to ~10 Phase 3 obesity trials** via the Metsera platform in 2026.

**Why it matters:** Pfizer's re-entry into obesity after the danuglipron failure; differentiation play vs. Lilly/Novo.

Sources: [Pfizer JPM 2026 fireside chat deck (PDF)](https://s206.q4cdn.com/795948973/files/doc_events/2026/Jan/12/JPM-2026-Fireside-Chat-Presentation-FINAL.pdf); [BioPharma Dive](https://www.biopharmadive.com/news/jpm26-china-biotech-pfizer-obesity-bristol-sarepta-elevidys-sales/809320/).

## 6. Amgen — MariTide (maridebart cafraglutide) Phase 2 update

- Monday-evening reveal of **Phase 2 obesity** and **Phase 2 T2D** data on the once-monthly **GLP-1/GIPR-antagonist peptibody**.
- T2D arm: clinically meaningful HbA1c and body-weight reductions at 24 weeks on monthly dosing; favorable cardiometabolic profile.
- Tolerability consistent with GLP-1 RA class; **six global Phase 3 studies** underway.

**Why it matters:** Reinforces Amgen's positioning as a third obesity major behind Lilly and Novo; monthly dosing as differentiator.

Source: [Clinical Trials Arena](https://www.clinicaltrialsarena.com/news/jpm26-amgen-maritide-obesity-t2d-phase-ii/).

## 7. BridgeBio — Attruby Q4 commercial beat

- Preliminary Q4 2025 Attruby (acoramidis) net revenue of **$146M**, ahead of consensus; +35% QoQ.
- **6,629 unique patient prescriptions** from 1,632 prescribers as of Dec 31, 2025.
- Leerink raised PT to $75; Morgan Stanley initiated Overweight with $96 PT.

**Why it matters:** Validates ATTR-CM commercial trajectory and BridgeBio's transition to revenue-generating biotech.

Source: [BridgeBio investor release](https://investor.bridgebio.com/news/news-details/2026/BridgeBio-Announces-Commercial-Progress-Program-Updates-and-2026-Milestones-at-the-44th-Annual-J-P--Morgan-Healthcare-Conference/default.aspx).

## 8. Sarepta — Elevidys re-commitment, prelim 2025 sales

- CEO Doug Ingram doubled down on **DMD gene therapy Elevidys** despite 2025 safety controversies (two patient deaths, FDA back-and-forth).
- Preliminary 2025 total sales: **$1.86B**; Elevidys contributed **$898.7M**.

**Why it matters:** Sets the tone for Sarepta's recovery narrative entering 2026.

Source: [BioPharma Dive](https://www.biopharmadive.com/news/jpm26-china-biotech-pfizer-obesity-bristol-sarepta-elevidys-sales/809320/).

## 9. Biogen — high-dose Spinraza approved by European Commission

- EC marketing authorization for **high-dose nusinersen** (50 mg/5 mL and 28 mg/5 mL) in 5q SMA, granted same day as Biogen's JPM presentation.
- Backed by DEVOTE study data in treatment-naïve and previously-treated patients. Already approved in Japan; **FDA action by April 3, 2026**.

**Why it matters:** Shores up an aging franchise on the morning Biogen presented its "new Biogen" pivot; CEO Chris Viehbacher emphasized continued immunology build-out via HI-Bio.

Source: [Biogen press release](https://investors.biogen.com/news-releases/news-release-details/biogen-receives-european-commission-approval-high-dose-regimen).

## 10. Moderna — 2025 sales beat, 2026 growth guide

- Preliminary unaudited 2025 sales of **$1.9B** (~$100M above midpoint of guidance).
- 2026 outlook: **up to ~10% sales growth** on a "large seasonal vaccine franchise" plus pipeline progression (CEO Stéphane Bancel).

**Why it matters:** Steadies the Moderna investor narrative post-COVID-revenue cliff.

Source: [STAT JPM 2026 Day 1 recap](https://www.statnews.com/2026/01/12/jpm-2026-day-1-top-stories-bridgebio-moderna-eli-lilly-readout-newsletter/).

## Themes emerging by end of Day 1

- **AI/bio convergence as headline theme** — Lilly–NVIDIA $1B lab framed Day 1; AI also dominated health-tech presentations (Waystar agentic AI).
- **Licensing displacing M&A** — Day 1 produced ~**$7.3B in announced licensing value** (AbbVie/RemeGen, Novartis/SciNeuro) but **zero megadeals**; multiple commentators flagged a "slow Monday."
- **Obesity remains the gravitational center** — Pfizer (Metsera), Amgen (MariTide), and the broader Lilly/Novo backdrop drove a disproportionate share of Day 1 narrative.
- **China-origin assets continue moving west** — RC148 (RemeGen) and the SciNeuro brain-shuttle platform both originated in China.
