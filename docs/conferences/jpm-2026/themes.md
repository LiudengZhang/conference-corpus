# Themes & takeaways — JPM 2026

Cross-day synthesis of the recurring threads, surprises, and named entities across [Day 1](digest/day-1.md), [Day 2](digest/day-2.md), [Day 3](digest/day-3.md), and [Day 4](digest/day-4.md).

## The five threads

### 1. AI in pharma — from partnership to ownership

The single loudest theme. JPM26 marked a phase change from "we have an AI collab" to "we own the AI company":

- **Lilly + NVIDIA $1B AI Co-Innovation Lab** (Day 1) — largest pharma–AI infrastructure commitment ever announced at JPM; NVIDIA BioNeMo + Vera Rubin compute.
- **AstraZeneca acquires Modella AI** (Day 2) — described in trade press as the first big-pharma acquisition of an AI firm; multi-modal foundation models for oncology.
- **Pfizer** announced plans to "embed AI across the business" (Day 2).
- **Waystar** projected RCM TAM expanding from $20–25B to **$55B with agentic AI** (Day 1).

The closing-week framing in Alex Hogan's STATus Report: AI is "**table stakes**" — the conversation has shifted from *will you do AI* to *show us the ROI and the compliance story* (echoed by Hogan Lovells on AI compliance + BIOSECURE).

**Counterweight:** **Regeneron's George Yancopoulos** publicly questioned current AI applications in medicine on Day 4 — calling for careful oversight. The conference's most prominent scientific voice pushing back on AI maximalism.

### 2. Licensing displacing M&A

JPM26 had ~**$8.3B in announced deals** (Pharmaceutical Technology) but **no pharma megamerger**. The pattern:

- **AbbVie + RemeGen $5.6B** PD-1×VEGF licensing (Day 1) — biggest single deal headline of the week.
- **Novartis + SciNeuro $1.7B** Alzheimer's antibody pact (Day 1).
- **Novartis + Zonsen PepLib $50M** radioligand license (Day 2).
- **AZ + Modella AI** — undisclosed terms, tuck-in (Day 2).
- **Boston Scientific + Penumbra $14.5B** (Day 4) — the *one* megadeal, and it's medtech, not biopharma.
- Pre-JPM **Lilly + Ventyx $1B+** acquisition cited as the largest biopharma deal near the conference window.

Fierce Biotech canonical framing: "**All the meetings, none of the megadeals.**" One attendee told Fierce some companies appear to be **holding announcements for the TD Cowen Health Care Conference in March**.

### 3. China is the story

Two distinct angles converged:

- **China-origin assets in big-pharma deals** — RC148 (RemeGen → AbbVie, Day 1), brain-shuttle Alzheimer's antibodies (SciNeuro → Novartis, Day 1), peptide radioligand (Zonsen PepLib → Novartis, Day 2).
- **Chinese pharma stage time** — Hengrui presented on Day 4 with **>$3.2B 2025 sales through Q3, 21 NDA/BLA approvals, $1.5B HK IPO, ~$16B cumulative out-licensing** (incl. GSK partnership). Kelun-Biotech presented bispecific/multispecific conjugates.

**BioPharma Dive's framing:** US biotech's "**Sputnik moment**." **Novartis CMO Shreeram Aradhye** (Day 3) cautioned against simple speed comparisons — different ecosystem, trial-enrollment economics, payer structure — but did not dispute the trend.

### 4. Obesity = the gravitational center

Every major pharma had to address it:

- **Pfizer** (Day 1, Day 2) — $10B Metsera acquisition anchors **10 Phase 3 starts in 2026**; Bourla compared cash-pay obesity demand to **Viagra**.
- **Amgen** — **MariTide** Phase 2 reveal (Day 1, reprise Day 3): up to 20% mean weight loss in obesity-only, 17% in obesity + T2D; potential first **once-monthly T2D therapy**; six global Phase 3 underway.
- **Novo Nordisk** (Day 2, Day 3) — **oral Wegovy ~3,100 patients in first week** since Jan 5 launch; Doustdar publicly hungry for M&A "with intention" after losing Metsera bid; pulling back from non-obesity/diabetes indications.
- **Roche** (Day 2) — teased entry into obesity as one of three growth pillars.
- The competition has shifted from drug-data tournament to **commercial/distribution warfare**: oral GLP-1s, monthly dosing, cash-pay channels.

### 5. Policy / regulatory headwinds as constant background noise

Threaded through every day:

- **Sanofi Day 3** — RSV recommendation narrowing on **Beyfortus**, recent FDA rejection, MFN pricing arrangement skirting tariff threats; Sanofi singled out the Dec 24, 2025 CRL on **tolebrutinib** for "skipped advisory committee" reasons.
- **Pazdur Day 2** — first public remarks since departing FDA, warning on politicization and voucher-program transparency.
- **Mehmet Oz Day 2** — fielded hospital-exec questions on Medicaid and vaccines off-site.
- **Trump MFN pricing** — Yancopoulos (Day 4) explained why Regeneron was the only one of 17 companies receiving presidential pricing letters that hadn't yet struck a deal.
- **AI compliance + BIOSECURE** — Hogan Lovells flagged as top device-supply-chain concerns.

## Predictions to watch

From the closing recaps and threaded through the keynotes:

1. **AI bake-off goes from collab to acquisition.** Expect more big-pharma + AI-startup *acquisitions* (vs. partnerships) in 2026 following the AZ–Modella template.
2. **The TD Cowen Health Care Conference (March)** may absorb the megadeals JPM26 didn't see — at least one Fierce attendee suggested companies were holding announcements.
3. **Up to ~20 biotech IPOs in 2026** if the Sionna/Maze/Eikon/Aktis/Veradermics windows hold (HSBC's Jonathan Norris). 2025 had <12 IPOs, the lowest since at least 2018.
4. **Obesity competition shifts to distribution.** Novo's DTP/oral-Wegovy push, Amgen's once-monthly differentiation, Pfizer–Metsera's combos — the next 18 months are commercial, not data.
5. **Policy risk is structural, not transient.** Vaccine politics, MFN pricing letters, BIOSECURE, FDA-process scrutiny — all four will continue weighing on biopharma narratives through 2026.
6. **China-origin licensing is now mainstream.** Three of the four top deals at JPM26 had Chinese-origin assets; expect this share to grow.
