## Track shortlist — most-loaded tracks

### Lymphoma (DLBCL, FL, MCL, CLL, T-cell)

The single largest disease axis at ASH.

- **DLBCL bispecifics — durability + earlier-line.** Epcoritamab (EPCORE NHL-2 / -3 / -5 long-term), glofitamab (post-CD20×CD3 combos), mosunetuzumab (FL → DLBCL extensions). The class-of-2024 is now a class-of-2026 with 3-yr durability data. 1L combinations (epco-R-CHOP, glofit-Pola-R-CHP) are the new front.
- **Post-CAR-T salvage.** Bispecific-after-CAR-T as the de facto salvage standard. Real-world registry data (CIBMTR, LYSA, GLA cohorts) frame the absolute numbers.
- **CLL — pirtobrutinib + venetoclax fixed-duration.** BRUIN CLL-321 in covalent-BTKi-resistant CLL is the pivotal expected. Fixed-duration ven combinations (MAJIC, CLL17) frame the frontline.
- **Mantle cell + indolent.** MCL pirtobrutinib / brexucabtagene autoleucel long-term; FL mosunetuzumab + lenalidomide.

### Myeloma

Second largest disease axis. Bispecific / CAR-T crowd.

- **CARTITUDE-5 / -6** (J&J/Legend cilta-cel frontline pivots) — the frontline CAR-T moment.
- **KarMMa-9** (BMS ide-cel frontline) — parallel frontline pivot.
- **MajesTEC-3 / -5** (J&J teclistamab combinations) — bispecific + dara / pom / len frontline pivots.
- **MonumenTAL-3** (J&J talquetamab pivotal).
- **RedirecTT-1 / -2** (dual bispecific BCMA + GPRC5D in EMD MM) — class watch.
- **Allogeneic anti-BCMA CAR-T** (Allogene, Caribou, others) — manufacturing / off-the-shelf class watch.

The dominant clinical question across the track: **sequencing — bispecific-after-CAR-T vs CAR-T-after-bispecific**; multiple real-world registries will speak to it.

### Acute leukemias

- **AML menin (revumenib, ziftomenib) post-approval.** Both approved late 2024 / 2025. Real-world + combos with ven/aza, FLT3i, IDHi. Frontline pivots beginning.
- **AML maintenance.** VIALE-M (venetoclax maintenance post-induction) + others.
- **FLT3, IDH frontline.** Gilteritinib, quizartinib, ivosidenib, enasidenib, olutasidenib — frontline + maintenance pivots.
- **B-ALL CAR-T.** Adult ALL CAR-T (brexu-cel, tisa-gen) durability; pediatric updates.

### MDS / MPN

- **Imetelstat post-IMerge real-world** (lower-risk MDS TI).
- **Luspatercept frontline ESA-naive** (COMMANDS long-term).
- **IDH inhibitors in MDS** (Servier).
- **Myelofibrosis** — momelotinib, pacritinib post-approval; selinexor combos.

### Cell-and-gene therapy axis (cross-cutting)

ASH owns this story; no other heme meeting carries equal weight.

- **CAR-T 5-yr durability** for axi-cel (Kite), liso-cel (BMS), cilta-cel (J&J/Legend), ide-cel (BMS/2seventy). The "CAR-T tail" — fraction still in remission at 5+ yr — is the load-bearing curve for the entire class economics.
- **SPM (second-primary-malignancy) signal.** FDA reporting + CIBMTR registry data. The toxicity-class-action question.
- **Gene therapy for SCD.** Exa-cel / Casgevy (Vertex/CRISPR) and lovo-cel / Lyfgenia (bluebird) 3+ yr post-approval data. Pediatric pivots. Access / payer data.
- **In-vivo gene editing for SCD.** Beam, Editas, Verve, Prime Medicine pipelines. Class watch — early-phase.
- **Hemophilia gene therapy.** Roctavian (BioMarin val-rox) and Hemgenix (CSL etranadez) 2–4 yr FVIII / FIX expression curves. The durability question.

### Non-malignant heme

- **Hemophilia non-factor agents.** Mim8 (Novo, FVIIIa-mimetic post-FRONTIER), concizumab (Novo), fitusiran (Sanofi) — bleed-rate pivots and long-term.
- **iTTP / TTP.** Caplacizumab + rituximab follow-up.
- **PNH.** Pegcetacoplan, iptacopan, danicopan — C5 vs C3 inhibitor head-to-heads / combinations.
- **Sickle cell non-gene-therapy.** Voxelotor (post-withdrawal), crizanlizumab follow-up, etavopivat.
- **Thrombosis & hemostasis.** Factor XI inhibitors (abelacimab, milvexian, asundexian) — post-OCEANIC / LIBREXIA / OCEANIC-AF data. Cross-link to cardiology readouts at AHA Nov 7–10, 2026.

## EHA 2026 reconciliation (mid-year)

**EHA 2026 Hybrid Congress** is **June 11–14, 2026 in Milan, Italy** (~6 weeks from today). EHA is the European equivalent and frequently presents heme-cancer readouts that update at ASH six months later. Capture EHA-first readouts in mid-June and tag them in `trials/` as "EHA 2026 readout, ASH 2026 update expected." This drives a substantial fraction of the ASH pre-meeting framing.

Typical EHA → ASH update patterns:
- EHA presents 1- or 2-yr update; ASH presents 3-yr update.
- EHA presents phase 2 cohort; ASH presents phase 3.
- EHA presents European subset; ASH presents global cohort.
