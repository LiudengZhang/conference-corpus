## Thesis

Flexible, community-driven software is the multiplier on genomic research — but "flexible" has a specific operational meaning: composable packages, low-friction authoring of teaching material, reproducible compute substrates, and a clear path from a research script to a vignette to a book to a reusable workflow. As the BiocBook author, Serizay is positioned to argue that the *authoring layer* — Quarto-shaped, Bioconductor-native — is the part of the ecosystem most directly responsible for whether community contributions compound or fragment.

## Speaker context

Jacques Serizay runs (or co-runs) work at Institut Pasteur on chromatin organization and regulatory genomics, with an unusually heavy software output for a wet-lab-adjacent group: he is the author of `BiocBook`, the Quarto-based authoring framework that produces Bioconductor-shaped books (used for OSTA and several other recent titles), and a contributor to the `tidyomics` / chromatin-tools landscape. The closing-keynote slot for a software-author rather than a methods-author is a deliberate framing choice — it puts the *infrastructure of how we share methods* on equal footing with the methods themselves.
