# Day 3 — Friday Sep 19, 2025

Day 3 was a half-day designed for synthesis and community: one keynote, a flash-talk reel, Birds-of-a-Feather huddles, and the closing. After two front-loaded days of new packages and dense short-talk sessions, the format shifted from broadcast to conversation — which is where Bioconductor as a project actually maintains itself.

## Morning

Jacques Serizay's [Enhancing genomic research with community-driven flexible software](../keynotes/serizay.md) anchored the day. The title is the meeting's closing thesis: Bioconductor's value isn't any single package, it's the *coordination mechanism* — peer review, release cycles, vignette standards, books, workshop platforms — that lets ~2,300 packages cohere. Serizay's own work on BiocBook and tidy-genomics packages embodies the argument.

The 09:30 flash-talk block followed — short pitches that typically map onto poster-session highlights and previews of next year's short talks. (Specific flash talks to be filled in once the program is cross-checked against the recap; treat each as a candidate to fold into the tools index if a package emerges.)

## Afternoon

The 11:15 [Birds-of-a-Feather sessions](../keynotes/serizay.md#birds-of-a-feather) were the day's working session — small-group huddles around topics the community self-organizes (typical themes at past EuroBioCs: spatial-omics standardization, single-cell interoperability, training and onboarding, infra/devops, multi-omics integration). BoFs are where the next year's working groups, joint reviews, and cross-package coordinations actually start. They don't show up in the recap photos but they are often where the meeting's biggest medium-term outcomes originate.

The 12:45 closing wrapped the meeting — typical agenda: thanks, awards, next-meeting handoff (BioC2026 location announcement, GBCC2025 cross-promotion).

## Posters & social

Day 3 had no formal poster slot — the second poster session on Day 2 was the last. The closing lunch and informal hallway conversations served the social function.
