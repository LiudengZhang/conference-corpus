# ESHG 2026

**59th European Human Genetics Conference** — Svenska Mässan (Swedish Exhibition & Congress Centre), Gothenburg, Sweden · June 13 – 16, 2026 (hybrid; pre-conference courses Jun 11 – 12). ~3,000 attendees expected — the European counterpart to ASHG and the largest human-genetics meeting in Europe.

## Why this is in the vault

ESHG is the **European twin to ASHG** and the venue where European biobank readouts, the Nordic / Karolinska clinical-genetics network, and the German / Dutch / UK rare-disease consortia present first. The overlap with the cancer-genomics corpus is asymmetric but load-bearing, and the European angle (single-payer health systems, EU GDPR-shaped data governance, EMA pharmacogenomics framing) gives ESHG a different read on the same biology than ASHG:

1. **Hereditary cancer in European cohorts.** BRCA / Lynch / Li-Fraumeni cohort studies anchored in European registries (Dutch HEBON, Swedish SweBCG, UK CanRisk, German GC-HBOC) present first at ESHG. Variant-classification debates at ESHG seed ClinVar updates downstream.
2. **European biobank readouts.** FinnGen (Finland, ~520k participants), UK Biobank (~500k), Generation R (Rotterdam), GeneRISK (Finnish polygenic-risk pilot), Estonian Biobank, deCODE — all first-class at ESHG. Cancer-relevant subsets (cancer-PRS, hereditary-cancer prevalence, somatic mosaicism) anchor the methods feeding AACR / ASCO downstream.
3. **Pharmacogenomics and clinical implementation.** Ubiquitous-PGx rollouts in the Nordic countries, the Netherlands, and the UK are an ESHG specialty. Drug-gene pair adoption (DPYD, TPMT, CYP2D6, UGT1A1) for oncology drugs has clearer European clinical-pathway data than US.
4. **AI in clinical genomics — EU AI Act framing.** Foundation models for variant effect, dysmorphology AI, and AI-assisted prenatal screening all carry an EU regulatory overlay at ESHG that ASHG largely doesn't surface.
5. **ELSI / ELPAG.** The Ethics and Public Policy thread at ESHG (ELPAG track + dedicated Award Lecture) is proportionally larger than ASHG ELSI, with GDPR / EU AI Act / cross-border biobanking as recurring axes.

**Hybrid template (and the design decision behind this vault):** ESHG, like ASHG, is **neither pure-clinical (ASCO) nor pure-tools (EuroBioC)** — most program slots are "investigator-talk anchored on a study + method + cohort," so the unit of analysis is **the talk**, not the trial and not the tool. The closest sibling shape in the vault is [ASHG 2026](../ashg-2026/index.md), which we mirror directly — single `talks/` directory with a schema that accommodates tool-anchored / clinical-study-anchored / methods-anchored / award-lecture entries. For ESHG we additionally tag European-cohort talks (FinnGen / UKB / Generation R / GeneRISK / Estonian Biobank / deCODE) because that's the differentiator from ASHG.

## Programme shape

ESHG 2026 runs **Saturday June 13 (opening plenary 14:00) through Tuesday June 16 (closing 16:00)**, four days, with **pre-conference courses Thursday Jun 11 – Friday Jun 12** (notably the "Clinical NGS Data Interpretation Course"). Svenska Mässan, Gothenburg. Hybrid (in-person + virtual). Structure (from the public Programme-at-a-Glance):

- **PL1 — Opening Plenary**, Sat Jun 13, 14:00–15:45. Welcome + opening lectures.
- **PL2–PL6 — Plenary Sessions**, distributed across Sun Jun 14 and Mon Jun 15 mornings. Mendel Lecture, ESHG Award Lecture, ELPAG Award Lecture, Young Investigator Awards (winners present in plenary slots; recipients not yet announced in public materials).
- **PL7 — Closing Awards Session**, Tue Jun 16, 15:20–16:00. Awards summary + closing remarks.
- **Educational Sessions (E01–E07)** — clinical NGS interpretation, AI in functional genomics, disease modeling, newborn screening, long-read sequencing, ELSI in AI genomics, **extrachromosomal DNA (ecDNA)** (cancer-relevant).
- **Interactive Sessions (I01–I18)** — episignatures, dysmorphology (two sessions), genome browsers, pharmacogenomics, structural variants, reproductive carrier screening, genetic counseling tools.
- **Concurrent Symposia (S01–S26)** — GWAS, chromosome X genetics, **rare cancers**, repeat expansions, prenatal testing, **polygenic scores**, **cancer mechanisms**, gene editing, evolutionary genomics, health economics in genomic medicine.
- **Concurrent Sessions (C01–C41)** — carrier screening, intellectual disability, **cancer research**, metabolic disorders, neurogenetics, emerging therapeutic approaches.
- **Pre-conference courses (Jun 11 – 12)** — full-day Clinical NGS Data Interpretation Course; consortium / society satellite meetings.
- **Abstract scale:** ~3,000+ submitted, with several hundred orals across concurrent symposia + sessions, plus poster halls.

## What's different from ASHG

- **European biobanks first-class.** FinnGen, UK Biobank, Generation R, GeneRISK, Estonian Biobank, deCODE updates anchor the cohort-talk slate. ASHG carries these too but ESHG carries them with more clinical-implementation depth (single-payer system pathways, national-cohort registry overlays).
- **Clinical-genetics weight.** ESHG runs proportionally more dysmorphology, prenatal, carrier-screening, and genetic-counseling content than ASHG. The "Dysmorphology Interactive Sessions" are a signature ESHG format.
- **EU regulatory overlay.** GDPR, EU AI Act, IVDR, EMA pharmacogenomics framing all surface throughout ESHG sessions. ELPAG is the ELSI track and has its own Award Lecture; the ELSI proportion of the programme is higher than ASHG ELSI.
- **Smaller scale.** ~3,000 attendees vs ~7,500 at ASHG. Fewer parallel platform sessions, but density of European-cohort + clinical-implementation content is higher per session.
- **June timing.** ESHG runs concurrent with the spring oncology cycle ([ASCO 2026](../asco-2026/index.md) May 29 – Jun 2 and [EHA 2026](../eha-2026/index.md) Jun 11 – 14), so cancer-genomics overlap with the same week's heme readouts is unusually tight. EHA 2026 (Stockholm) and ESHG 2026 (Gothenburg) are **both in Sweden the same week** — Sweden is the European-oncology / genomics hub for mid-June 2026.
- **Open-access disposition.** EJHG (Nature Portfolio) and HGG Advances (Cell Press) are both hybrid open-access. Citability is high. Abstract supplement has DOIs per entry.
- **Pre-conference courses.** ESHG runs a flagship Clinical NGS Data Interpretation Course (Jun 11–12) that doubles as the European clinical-laboratory training event for the year — load-bearing for clinical-implementation talks during the main meeting.

## Cross-vault links

- **[ASHG 2026](../ashg-2026/index.md)** — the US-side twin. Hereditary-cancer panel design, gnomAD, cancer-PRS, foundation-model variant-effect predictors all show up at both meetings (ESHG first in June, ASHG with longer datasets in October). Many investigators present at both.
- **[AACR 2026](../aacr-2026/index.md)** — cancer-biology / mechanism side. Hereditary cancer panels, germline-somatic integration, cancer-PRS investigators overlap. Rare-cancers symposium (S-track) at ESHG aligns with AACR rare-cancer sessions.
- **[ASCO 2026](../asco-2026/index.md)** — clinical translation (May 29 – Jun 2, immediately before ESHG). Cancer-PRS, hereditary-cancer-informed trial stratification, pharmacogenomics-guided oncology dosing — methods that surface at ESHG inform the European arm of trials reported at ASCO.
- **[EHA 2026](../eha-2026/index.md)** — runs Jun 11 – 14 in Stockholm, same week, same country. Clonal hematopoiesis at population scale and gene-therapy genomics (sickle cell / beta-thalassemia) tie ESHG cohort talks to EHA clinical readouts. Cross-attendance is feasible (Gothenburg ↔ Stockholm ~3.5h train).
- **[ASH 2026](../ash-2026/index.md)** — December follow-on for the CHIP / hematology bridge.
- **[ISMB 2026](../ismb-2026/index.md) / [RECOMB 2026](../recomb-2026/index.md) / [ECCB 2026](../eccb-2026/index.md)** — methods overlap (variant calling, deep-learning variant-effect predictors, foundation models for genomics). ECCB 2026 (Aug 31 – Sep 4) is the closest methods-meeting sibling.
- **[AGBT 2026](../agbt-2026/index.md)** — platform-launch overlap. Long-read clinical sequencing platforms that debut at AGBT in February show up applied (Clinical NGS course + S/C-track talks) at ESHG in June.
- **[EuroBioC 2025 tools](../eurobioc-2025/tools/index.md)** — Bioconductor packages used in ESHG analyses (statistical-genetics tooling, multi-omics, single-cell). Many European Bioconductor maintainers present applied work at ESHG.
- **[Conferences timeline](../../timeline.md)** — ESHG's position in the year's genomics cycle (AGBT Feb → AACR Apr → ASCO May/Jun → **ESHG + EHA Jun** → ISMB Jul → ESMO/ASHG Oct → SITC Nov → ASH Dec → JPM Jan).
