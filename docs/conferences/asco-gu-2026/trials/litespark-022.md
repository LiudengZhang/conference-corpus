# LITESPARK-022 — Adjuvant pembrolizumab + belzutifan vs pembrolizumab monotherapy in high-risk clear cell RCC

> **Abstract:** LBA418 · **Session:** Oral Abstract Session — RCC, Day 1 (Thu Feb 26, 2026), Moscone West · **Embargo:** lifted on presentation

**Headline:** Adding belzutifan to adjuvant pembrolizumab cut the risk of disease recurrence or death by 28% (DFS HR 0.72, 95% CI 0.59–0.87, p=0.0003) in resected high-risk ccRCC — the first IO + HIF-2α adjuvant combination to clear a Ph3 primary endpoint.

## At a glance

- **Sponsor:** Merck
- **PI / presenter:** Toni Choueiri (Dana-Farber Cancer Institute)
- **NCT ID:** NCT05239728
- **Phase:** 3
- **Design:** Double-blind, 1:1 randomization, adjuvant pembro + belzutifan vs pembro + placebo for up to ~17 cycles post-nephrectomy
- **N:** ~1,600 randomized, high-risk ccRCC (post-nephrectomy ± metastasectomy)
- **Primary endpoint(s):** DFS by investigator assessment
- **Comparator:** Pembrolizumab monotherapy (the post-KEYNOTE-564 adjuvant SOC)
- **Indication:** High-risk clear cell RCC, post-nephrectomy
- **Line:** Adjuvant

## Headline result

DFS HR 0.72 (95% CI 0.59–0.87, p=0.0003), reported at the protocol-specified interim. OS event count immature; trend reportedly favors the combination but not formally tested. Safety: known belzutifan anemia / hypoxia signal additive with pembro; treatment discontinuation rates manageable, no new safety signals.

## Mechanism / class

Belzutifan: oral HIF-2α inhibitor, first-in-class. Approved 2021 for VHL-associated tumors; expanded to 3L+ advanced ccRCC (LITESPARK-005) in 2023. Pembrolizumab adjuvant established by KEYNOTE-564 (DFS positive 2021; OS positive ESMO 2024). LITESPARK-022 combines two mechanistically complementary classes — checkpoint inhibition + hypoxia-pathway disruption.

## Discussant takeaway

Discussant (Robert J. Motzer, MSK) framed it as the "second pillar" of adjuvant ccRCC after KEYNOTE-564 — but cautioned that the absolute DFS benefit is modest in a population where pembro monotherapy is already effective. David McDermott (BIDMC) emphasized the tolerability question for an ~12-month adjuvant course.

## Practice-changing verdict

**Yes — with caveats.** Merck's sNDA was already under FDA priority review at time of GU readout, with PDUFA action anticipated mid-2026. NCCN kidney guideline anticipated to add the combination as a preferred adjuvant option following FDA action. Open question: OS magnitude — the DFS-to-OS conversion for adjuvant ccRCC has historically been uncertain (KEYNOTE-564 took ~5 years for OS).
