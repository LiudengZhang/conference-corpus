# PEACE-3 / EORTC 1333 — Enzalutamide + radium-223 vs enzalutamide alone in bone-metastatic mCRPC

> **Abstract:** Oral · **Session:** Oral Abstract Session — Prostate, Day 3 (Sat Feb 28, 2026), Moscone West · **Embargo:** lifted on presentation

**Headline:** Adding radium-223 to enzalutamide cut the risk of death by 24% (OS HR 0.76, 95% CI 0.60–0.96, one-sided p=0.0096) in bone-mets mCRPC — re-validates the alpha-emitter + ARPI combination with modern bone-protective agents and follows the positive rPFS readout at ESMO 2024.

## At a glance

- **Sponsor:** EORTC (with Bayer / Astellas drug supply)
- **PI / presenter:** Silke Gillessen (IOSI, Bellinzona) — presented the OS readout
- **NCT ID:** NCT02194842
- **Phase:** 3
- **Design:** Open-label, 1:1 randomization, enzalutamide + radium-223 vs enzalutamide alone, with mandatory bone-protective agent (denosumab or zoledronic acid)
- **N:** ~446 randomized
- **Primary endpoint(s):** rPFS (read out ESMO 2024); key secondary OS (this readout)
- **Comparator:** Enzalutamide monotherapy
- **Indication:** Bone-metastatic mCRPC, asymptomatic / mildly symptomatic, 1L mCRPC setting
- **Line:** 1L mCRPC (post-ADT, may include prior docetaxel for mHSPC)

## Headline result

OS HR 0.76 (95% CI 0.60–0.96, one-sided p=0.0096). Median OS 42.3 vs 35.0 months (post-meeting trade-press summary). The prior rPFS readout at ESMO 2024 reported HR 0.69 (median rPFS 19.4 vs 16.4 months). Critically, fracture rates were similar between arms because of mandatory bone-protective therapy — addressing the safety concern that sank the ERA-223 (abi + Ra-223) combination in 2018.

## Mechanism / class

Radium-223 (Xofigo): alpha-emitting calcium mimetic, targets osteoblastic bone metastases. Approved 2013 for symptomatic CRPC bone-mets monotherapy (ALSYMPCA OS positive). Enzalutamide: 2nd-gen androgen receptor inhibitor. PEACE-3 is the first Ph3 to combine the alpha-emitter with an ARPI **and** rigorous bone protection — the design lesson learned from ERA-223.

## Discussant takeaway

Discussant (Karim Fizazi, Gustave Roussy) framed it as a "rehabilitation of radium-223" in the modern ARPI era. Stephen Freedland (Cedars-Sinai) emphasized the mandatory bone-protective agent as a non-negotiable protocol element. Open question: how this competes against ¹⁷⁷Lu-PSMA-617 (PSMAfore, PSMAaddition) for the same bone-mets niche.

## Practice-changing verdict

**Yes.** NCCN prostate update likely to elevate enza + Ra-223 (with bone-protective agent) as a Category 1 option in bone-mets mCRPC. Bayer expected to pursue a label expansion. ASCO 2026 main meeting will host a bone-mets subgroup follow-up.
