# IMvigor011 — ctDNA-guided adjuvant atezolizumab vs observation in ctDNA-positive MIBC

> **Abstract:** Oral · **Session:** Oral Abstract Session — Urothelial / Bladder, Day 2 (Fri Feb 27, 2026), Moscone West · **Embargo:** lifted on presentation
>
> *(Catch-all entry for the bladder ctDNA / emerging-readout slot in this vault; chosen over the SunRISE-2 negative readout and the rapid-orals FGFR3 entries as the single strongest emerging-bladder readout at ASCO GU 2026.)*

**Headline:** ctDNA-guided adjuvant atezolizumab improved DFS over observation in ctDNA-positive post-cystectomy MIBC patients — the first Ph3 to validate ctDNA surveillance as a **deployable** adjuvant trigger rather than a research-only biomarker.

## At a glance

- **Sponsor:** Roche / Genentech
- **PI / presenter:** Thomas Powles (Barts Cancer Institute)
- **NCT ID:** NCT04660344
- **Phase:** 3
- **Design:** Open-label, 1:1 randomization, atezolizumab vs observation, **only in patients who turned ctDNA-positive on serial monitoring** post-cystectomy (Signatera or comparable tumor-informed assay)
- **N:** ~495 randomized ctDNA+ patients (from a screened pool of ~1,200)
- **Primary endpoint(s):** DFS in ctDNA+ population; key secondary OS, ctDNA clearance rate
- **Comparator:** Observation (no adjuvant therapy — the prior SOC for ctDNA+ but radiographically NED patients)
- **Indication:** Muscle-invasive urothelial carcinoma (MIBC), post-cystectomy, ctDNA-positive on surveillance
- **Line:** Adjuvant

## Headline result

Positive DFS per topline. Trade-press synthesis: DFS HR ~0.5 range in the ctDNA+ population, with ~40% of treated patients achieving ctDNA clearance on atezo. The "watch-and-trigger" design — only treating ctDNA-positive patients — addresses the over-treatment problem of un-selected adjuvant IO (IMvigor010 was negative in all comers).

## Mechanism / class

Atezolizumab: anti-PD-L1 (Tecentriq). The mechanism is not the story here — IMvigor011 is a **design-driven** practice change, not a drug-driven one. The story is the ctDNA assay (tumor-informed, in this trial Signatera-anchored) as the patient-selection gate. The same paradigm read out positive in CRC adjuvant (DYNAMIC, GALAXY) and is in Ph3 in HR+ breast (LEADER, c-TRAK TN), but bladder MIBC is the first to land it for IO specifically.

## Discussant takeaway

Discussant (Andrea Apolo, NCI) framed it as the **proof-of-concept for ctDNA-guided adjuvant IO** — generalizes beyond bladder into the broader perioperative IO question (lung ALCHEMIST, melanoma adjuvant). Matthew Galsky emphasized that the assay infrastructure (Signatera turnaround, payer coverage) is now the gating issue, not the science.

## Practice-changing verdict

**Yes — for a defined subset.** NCCN bladder guidelines anticipated to add ctDNA-guided adjuvant atezo as an option for post-cystectomy ctDNA+ MIBC. Forward-link to NIAGARA biomarker analyses (also at this meeting) for the durvalumab read-through. The bigger field implication: ctDNA-guided adjuvant IO is now a real treatment paradigm, not a research curiosity.
