# AACR Brain Cancer 2026

**AACR Special Conference in Cancer Research: Brain Cancer** — Philadelphia Marriott Downtown, Philadelphia, PA · March 23 – 25, 2026. Subspecialty AACR special conference covering adult and pediatric brain tumors — glioblastoma, IDH-mutant glioma, medulloblastoma, brain metastases, and rare CNS subtypes — with strong neuroscience, neuro-immuno-oncology, and cellular-therapy threads.

> **Status:** Retrospective scaffold — meeting **closed seven weeks ago** (Mar 23–25, 2026; today is May 11, 2026). Vault is being seeded now to anchor the brain-tumor axis for the rest of the 2026 calendar (ASCO neuro-oncology track May 29 – Jun 2; SNO Annual Nov; SITC neuro-IO Nov 4–8). Sessions and speaker references inline are reconstructed from the public AACR program page, the Penn Medicine institutional roundup, chair-disclosed program-overview material (Bagley / Monje / Suva), and selected sponsor / institutional press; the AACR Brain 26 abstract supplement (in *Cancer Research*) is the citable record and is referenced where available. Pre-meeting topline content captured during the conference week is flagged inline.

## Why this is in the vault

AACR Brain Cancer is the **subspecialty AACR special conference for neuro-oncology** — the venue where the cancer-biology and neuroscience halves of the brain-tumor field meet in one program, in a year that does *not* have an SNO-only inflection. It sits at a deliberate intersection with several other axes already in the vault:

1. **AACR Annual Meeting mechanism axis** — many AACR Brain 26 chairs and speakers (Bagley UPenn, Monje Stanford, Suva Broad/MGH) also chair or speak in [AACR 2026](../aacr-2026/index.md) neuro-oncology and single-cell sessions four weeks later in San Diego (Apr 17–22). The "deep neuro biology" tracks at the special conference set up the abbreviated mechanism slots at Annual.
2. **Cell-therapy / IO axis (CAR-T in CNS)** — chlorotoxin-CAR-T (City of Hope), IL-13Rα2 + EGFR bivalent CAR-T (UPenn / CHOP / Stanford), GD2-CAR-T in DIPG and diffuse midline glioma (Monje group, Stanford), and chimeric-cytokine / armoured cell-therapy approaches. Same biology that anchors the cellular-therapy track at [SITC 2026](../sitc-2026/index.md) in November.
3. **Targeted-therapy axis (IDH-mutant glioma)** — vorasidenib (Voranigo, FDA-approved Aug 2024) is the first targeted therapy for grade 2 IDH-mutant astrocytoma / oligodendroglioma. INDIGO follow-up plus vorasidenib-combination signals (TTF, immune, radiation) thread through the program. Counterpart trials feed forward to [ASCO 2026](../asco-2026/index.md) and SNO 2026.
4. **Pediatric oncology / medulloblastoma axis** — molecular-subgroup-specific therapy (WNT, SHH, Group 3 / 4), CAR-T for relapsed disease, and DIPG / DMG cell-therapy. Strong CHOP / Stanford / St. Jude / DFCI representation.
5. **Single-cell / spatial neuro biology** — Suva, Tirosh, Bernstein, Patel-aligned labs at the discovery edge of glioma cell-state mapping, with direct methodological overlap with the [AACR 2026 single-cell / spatial topic](../aacr-2026/topics/single-cell-spatial-omics/index.md).

**Hybrid per-talk template (and the design decision behind this vault):** Brain Cancer 26 is **neither pure-clinical (ASCO) nor pure-tools (EuroBioC) nor pure-trial (SABCS)** — like SITC and ASHG, it programs neuroscience-mechanism, cellular-therapy clinical, targeted-therapy biology, and single-cell methods in the same room, often back-to-back. The unit of analysis is the **talk**, not the trial or the tool. We're using the ASHG-style hybrid per-talk template (see `talks/index.md`) with an explicit `Anchor type` field that resolves to one of: `cell therapy / trial`, `targeted therapy / trial`, `cohort / readout`, `mechanism / biology talk`, `tool / pipeline`, `award lecture`.

## Program shape

AACR Brain Cancer 2026 ran **Monday March 23 – Wednesday March 25, 2026**, three days, Philadelphia Marriott Downtown. Co-chaired by **Stephen J. Bagley** (UPenn), **Michelle Monje** (Stanford), and **Mario L. Suva** (Harvard / Broad / MGH). Structure followed the AACR special-conference pattern:

- **Plenary keynotes** — bookending the meeting. Mario Suva and Simona Parrinello (UCL) delivered named keynote talks; the latter on "The role of injury programs in gliomagenesis." Additional keynotes (TBA in our reconstruction) framed neuro-immuno-oncology and pediatric-CNS-tumor sessions.
- **Concurrent thematic sessions** — clustered around: GBM CAR-T and cellular therapy; IDH-mutant glioma biology and clinical; pediatric brain tumors (medulloblastoma molecular subgroups, DIPG / DMG); brain metastases (lung / breast / melanoma); neuroscience of gliomas (neural circuits, dopamine signaling, microglia, neurogliogenesis); blood-brain-barrier delivery; single-cell and spatial CNS tumor biology; tumor heterogeneity and resistance.
- **Poster sessions** — translational and discovery posters across all of the above; full abstract list on the AACR Brain 26 abstracts page.
- **Mentorship / career sessions** — AACR special conferences include an early-career investigator program.
- **Press / media** — limited press program; trade-press editorial recaps in the OncLive / Neurology Live / Targeted Oncology cadence.

**Tracks dominating the corpus** (per the program page + chair public statements):

- **Cellular therapy for CNS tumors** — CAR-T for GBM (chlorotoxin, IL-13Rα2 single-target, IL-13Rα2 + EGFR bivalent / bicistronic, EGFRvIII, B7-H3); CAR-T for medulloblastoma and DIPG / DMG (GD2, B7-H3); locoregional / intraventricular delivery; engineered T-cell biology (next-gen designs, TGF-β-resistant constructs, cell-state-directed engineering); endogenous-immune-system interplay.
- **IDH-mutant glioma** — vorasidenib (Voranigo) post-INDIGO biology and combinations; IDH-mut-specific immunotherapy (vaccine, neoantigen); 2-HG biology and downstream epigenetic targeting; high-grade IDH-mut clinical experience.
- **Glioblastoma biology** — cell-state plasticity (AC-/MES-/OPC-/NPC-like, per Suva / Tirosh / Bernstein single-cell framework); resistance mechanisms; intratumoral heterogeneity; clonal evolution under therapy.
- **Pediatric brain tumors** — medulloblastoma molecular subgroups (WNT / SHH / Group 3 / Group 4) and subgroup-specific therapy; DIPG and DMG (H3K27M, ONC201, GD2-CAR-T); ependymoma; ATRT; targetable fusion-driven low-grade glioma (BRAF / RAF / MEK; DAY101 / tovorafenib).
- **Brain metastases** — incidence trends (CNS-active ADCs, targeted-therapy effects on CNS pattern); HER2 brain mets (T-DXd CNS activity, tucatinib); melanoma + lung; leptomeningeal disease; intrathecal regimens.
- **Neuroscience of gliomas** — neural-circuit integration (Monje lab thread); activity-dependent glioma growth; dopamine / serotonin signaling in glioma; microglia and tumor-associated microglia / macrophages (TAM); astrocyte-glioma crosstalk; peripheral-nerve cancer biology.
- **Blood-brain-barrier penetration** — BBB / BTB-aware drug design; focused-ultrasound BBB opening; convection-enhanced delivery; PK / PD modelling for CNS-penetrant agents; ADC CNS activity question.
- **Single-cell and spatial CNS biology** — scRNA-seq / snRNA-seq atlases of glioma; spatial transcriptomics in glioma and pediatric brain tumors; integration with neuroscience markers; 3D / volumetric methods.
- **Tumor heterogeneity and 3D / spatial genomics** — strategies to reconcile bulk and single-cell readouts; 3D-genome organization in glioma.
- **Next-generation diagnostics / biomarkers** — CSF ctDNA / cfDNA in glioma and pediatric CNS tumors; methylation classifier (DKFZ MNP) updates; liquid biopsy for response monitoring.
- **Neuro-immuno-oncology beyond CAR-T** — checkpoint blockade in GBM (the persistent unmet niche), oncolytic virus, vaccine approaches.

## What's different from AACR Annual Meeting, SITC, and SABCS

- **Smaller, denser, more neuro-pure.** ~600–900 attendees vs AACR Annual's ~22,000. Every session is neuro-oncology; no breast, no GI, no GU, no non-cancer biology. Tighter cross-talk between cancer biologists, neuroscientists, and clinicians than at Annual.
- **More neuroscience than other special conferences.** The Monje thread (neural-circuit integration, activity-dependent glioma growth) and the microglia / TAM thread are bigger here than anywhere else in the cancer-meeting calendar. Closer to the Society for Neuroscience (SfN) end of the spectrum than SITC or SABCS.
- **Earlier-phase clinical than SABCS.** Most cell-therapy and targeted-therapy readouts here are Ph1/2 cohorts and dose-escalation updates, not randomized Ph3. The randomized vorasidenib INDIGO readout was at ASCO 2023 — by 2026 the work is biology, combinations, and post-marketing.
- **Pediatric component is substantial.** Unlike most adult-cancer meetings, the pediatric brain-tumor track is roughly co-equal with the adult-GBM track in airtime. Medulloblastoma and DIPG / DMG content is comparable in volume to glioblastoma content.
- **Single-cell biology load-bearing.** Suva / Tirosh / Bernstein / Patel-aligned labs essentially defined glioma single-cell biology; the special conference is where their lineage-and-state framework gets updated. Direct overlap with [AACR 2026 single-cell topic](../aacr-2026/topics/single-cell-spatial-omics/index.md).
