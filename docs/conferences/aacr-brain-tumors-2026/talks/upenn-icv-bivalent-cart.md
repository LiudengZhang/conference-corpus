# UPenn intracerebroventricular bivalent CAR-T (EGFR + IL-13Rα2) for recurrent GBM

**Ph1 update of locoregional bivalent EGFR + IL-13Rα2 CAR-T delivered into the CSF via Ommaya for adult recurrent glioblastoma.**

- **Speaker(s):** Stephen J. Bagley, MD, MSCE; co-investigators Donald M. O'Rourke, Carl H. June (lab and clinical team)
- **Affiliation:** Abramson Cancer Center / Perelman School of Medicine, University of Pennsylvania
- **Anchor type:** cell therapy / trial
- **Day / session:** Day 1 (Mon Mar 23, 2026), Cellular Therapy for GBM session; Bagley also co-chaired the meeting
- **Status:** post-meeting; details captured from AACR Brain 26 program notes, Penn Medicine institutional roundup, and the published Nature Medicine 2024 / PMC 2025 dataset that the cohort expansion extends; specific Brain 26 cohort numerics TBD pending *Cancer Research* abstract supplement

## Headline finding

Updated Ph1 cohort of locoregionally delivered, bicistronic CAR-T co-targeting EGFR and IL-13Rα2 in adult recurrent GBM, building on the Nature Medicine 2024 first-in-human report that documented rapid, transient tumor regressions in the index patients. The 2026 update reads out an expanded, ICV-dosed (intracerebroventricular, via Ommaya reservoir) cohort with longer follow-up. Exact ORR, durability, and DLT numbers from the AACR Brain 26 talk TBD pending the abstract supplement.

## Mechanism / methodology

A single bicistronic CAR construct co-expresses two scFvs — one against EGFR (wild-type and variant III) and one against IL-13Rα2 — paired to a 4-1BB costimulatory domain (per the Nature Medicine 2024 construct). Cells are delivered into the CSF via Ommaya reservoir rather than IV or intratumoral, exploiting CSF circulation to reach multifocal disease and limit on-target/off-tumor systemic EGFR toxicity. Dual antigen targeting is designed to blunt single-target antigen escape seen with EGFRvIII-only CAR-T.
