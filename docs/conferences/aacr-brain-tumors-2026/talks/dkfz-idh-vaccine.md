# DKFZ IDH-mutant glioma peptide / neoantigen vaccine update

**IDH1R132H peptide vaccine — the DKFZ-led neoantigen IO program for IDH-mutant glioma, extending NOA-16 toward combination and post-vorasidenib positioning.**

- **Status:** post-meeting; details captured from AACR Brain 26 program notes, the NOA-16 Nature 2021 first-in-human report (Platten et al.), and DKFZ follow-up; expanded 2026 cohort numerics and any vaccine + checkpoint combination data TBD pending abstract supplement

## Headline finding

Updated cohort and follow-on data from the DKFZ IDH1R132H peptide vaccine program — the most-watched neoantigen-IO + IDH-mut combination strategy. NOA-16 (Nature 2021) established that the IDH1R132H peptide is genuinely immunogenic and produces mutation-specific CD4 T-cell responses with a tolerable safety profile in newly-diagnosed grade 3/4 IDH-mut glioma. The Brain 26 readout extends cohort N and brings vaccine + checkpoint (PD-1/PD-L1) combination signals into view, plus the practical question of how to sequence with vorasidenib. Specific PFS, immune-response rate, and combo-cohort numbers TBD.

## Mechanism / methodology

The vaccine is a long-peptide formulation of the IDH1R132H neoepitope, presented through HLA-DR class II to drive a tumor-selective CD4 T-cell response (the IDH1R132H mutation is a shared, truncal driver, making it a near-universal target across IDH-mut glioma). Combination arms layer PD-1 or PD-L1 blockade on top of vaccination to overcome the strongly immunosuppressive D-2-HG-driven TME — a logic that intersects with vorasidenib's ability to lower 2-HG and potentially "open" the TME for IO.

## Open questions

- Does vorasidenib (2-HG suppression) genuinely improve the immune microenvironment enough to amplify vaccine response, or does it paradoxically reduce neoepitope presentation by de-differentiating less?
- Long-term durability of IDH1R132H-specific T-cell responses and whether boosting / heterologous prime-boost extends them remains an open question.
