# Simona Parrinello keynote — the role of injury programs in gliomagenesis

**Plenary keynote reframing glioma origin and progression in terms of wound-healing / injury-response transcriptional programs hijacked from normal CNS biology.**

- **Speaker(s):** Simona Parrinello, PhD
- **Affiliation:** UCL Cancer Institute (Samantha Dickson Brain Cancer Unit), University College London

## Headline finding

Plenary keynote arguing that glioblastoma growth, invasion, and recurrence are organized by injury-response / wound-healing transcriptional programs — the same programs that normal astrocytes, oligodendrocyte-lineage cells, and microglia run after CNS injury — co-opted by tumor cells and their TME. The framing places gliomagenesis on a continuum with reactive astrogliosis and CNS repair rather than treating it as a purely cell-autonomous oncogenic program, and predicts that surgery, radiation, and chemotherapy themselves create the injury context that drives a fraction of recurrence. Specific datasets and quantitative claims from the Brain 26 talk TBD.

## Mechanism / methodology

Mouse models of glioma initiation and recurrence, paired with single-cell and spatial profiling of human glioma specimens, used to identify injury-program transcription factors and downstream effectors active in tumor cells, reactive astrocytes, and TAMs. The Parrinello lab's prior work on perivascular niche and stem-like glioma cell biology feeds the model. The keynote frames neurosurgical resection cavities and radiation-injured peritumoral tissue as a permissive niche for residual disease.
