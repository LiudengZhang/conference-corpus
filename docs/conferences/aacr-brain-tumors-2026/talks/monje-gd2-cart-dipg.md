# GD2-CAR-T for pediatric DIPG and diffuse midline glioma — Stanford Ph1 update

**Practice-defining pediatric CAR-T cohort update: autologous GD2-targeted CAR-T (IV + intracerebroventricular) in H3K27M-mutant diffuse midline glioma and DIPG.**

- **Speaker(s):** Michelle Monje, MD, PhD (also Brain 26 co-chair and gave the neuroscience-of-glioma plenary thread); Crystal L. Mackall, MD (cell-therapy lead)
- **Affiliation:** Stanford University (Department of Neurology & Neurological Sciences; Center for Cancer Cell Therapy); Howard Hughes Medical Institute
- **Anchor type:** cell therapy / trial
- **Day / session:** Day 2 (Tue Mar 24, 2026), Pediatric / DIPG session
- **Status:** post-meeting; details captured from AACR Brain 26 program notes, prior Stanford Ph1 reports (Nature 2022 / Nature Medicine 2024 follow-ups), and Monje/Mackall public statements; expanded 2026 cohort numerics TBD pending abstract supplement

## Headline finding

Updated, expanded cohort of autologous GD2-CAR-T (4-1BB costim) in H3K27M-mutant DIPG and diffuse midline glioma (DMG), extending the earlier Stanford report (~9/10 evaluable clinical-benefit signal) into a larger multi-site Ph1 with longer survival follow-up and biomarker correlates. This remains the strongest CAR-T efficacy signal in any pediatric CNS solid tumor, in a disease (H3K27M DMG) where nothing has been practice-changing since ONC201 / dordaviprone emerged. Specific updated cohort N, PFS, and OS from the Brain 26 talk TBD.

## Mechanism / methodology

Autologous patient T cells transduced with a GD2-directed CAR (4-1BB / CD3ζ); GD2 is uniformly and highly expressed on H3K27M DMG. Initial cell dose is delivered intravenously after lymphodepletion, followed by intracerebroventricular re-dosing via Ommaya to address CNS pharmacokinetics. ICANS-class neuroinflammatory toxicity is managed proactively given tumor location in brainstem / thalamus. Biomarker workup includes CSF cytokine profiles, TCR-seq of expanded CAR-T product, and serial MRI volumetric response.
