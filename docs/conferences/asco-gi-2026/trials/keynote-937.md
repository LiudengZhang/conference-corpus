# KEYNOTE-937 — Adjuvant pembrolizumab post-curative-intent HCC

**Headline:** Adjuvant pembrolizumab after curative-intent resection or ablation of hepatocellular carcinoma failed to improve recurrence-free survival vs placebo. Negative readout — preserves active-surveillance SOC and is a meaningful counter-signal for the broader "perioperative IO extrapolation" thesis in liver cancer.

## At a glance

- **Sponsor / PI / institution:** Merck; presented by Pierre Gholam / Masatoshi Kudo (Kindai University) — KEYNOTE-937 investigators
- **NCT ID:** NCT03867084
- **Phase / arms / N:** Phase 3, randomized double-blind; pembrolizumab vs placebo for up to 17 cycles post-curative-intent therapy; N ~950
- **Primary endpoint(s):** RFS (BICR); OS key secondary
- **Indication / line:** Adjuvant HCC after surgical resection or local ablation with curative intent, intermediate/high recurrence risk
- **Comparator:** Placebo (over active surveillance)
- **Session:** Day 2 (Fri Jan 9, 2026), Hepatobiliary General Session, Moscone West

## Headline result

KEYNOTE-937 did not meet the primary RFS endpoint — pembrolizumab failed to demonstrate a statistically significant RFS improvement over placebo in the adjuvant HCC population. OS data immature at this analysis; safety consistent with known pembrolizumab profile. The result aligns with the prior IMbrave-050 atezo-bev adjuvant HCC negative readout (RFS positive at interim but did not hold; sponsor withdrew).

## Mechanism / class

Pembrolizumab is an anti-PD-1 monoclonal antibody. The adjuvant HCC hypothesis was extrapolated from advanced-HCC IO success (atezo-bev IMbrave150, durvalumab+treme HIMALAYA), where checkpoint blockade clearly improves OS in unresectable disease.

## Trial design deep dive

**Study architecture.** Phase 3 randomized 1:1 double-blind placebo-controlled; pembrolizumab 400 mg Q6W for up to 17 cycles (~2 years) post-curative-intent resection or local ablation. Stratification by recurrence-risk category (intermediate vs high based on tumor size, number, MVI), curative modality (resection vs ablation), and region (Asia vs ex-Asia). N ~950 globally.

**Endpoint definitions.** Primary endpoint RFS by BICR — defined as time from randomization to first documented recurrence (intra- or extra-hepatic) or death. OS was a key secondary tested hierarchically only if RFS hit. RFS censoring rules included loss to follow-up and start of new anti-cancer therapy. Sensitivity analyses included investigator-assessed RFS and recurrence-risk-stratified RFS. TBD on whether RFS event count was at protocol-specified maturity threshold.

**Key subgroup analyses.**

- **Intermediate vs high recurrence risk:** TBD — both subgroups failed to show benefit per topline; no enrichment for high-risk subset.
- **Curative modality (resection vs ablation):** TBD — sample sizes likely too small to declare a directional signal.
- **Etiology (HBV vs HCV vs NASH/alcohol):** of high mechanistic interest given different immune microenvironments; TBD on stratified HRs.
- **PD-L1 CPS / TMB:** exploratory biomarker analyses pending; no upfront biomarker enrichment in design.

**Comparator context.** Placebo (effectively active surveillance) — the prevailing post-curative HCC SOC. IMbrave-050 (atezo + bev) was the prior adjuvant HCC Phase 3, with positive interim RFS that did not hold at update; sponsor withdrew the supplementary indication. KEYNOTE-937 is the second consecutive negative adjuvant-IO HCC Phase 3.

**FDA / regulatory implications.** No filing planned for adjuvant HCC indication. Merck retains pembrolizumab approvals in advanced HCC (post-sorafenib monotherapy and in combination with lenvatinib via LEAP-002 framework). Negative readout reinforces FDA caution on perioperative-IO label extensions in HCC.

## Discussant takeaway

Hepatobiliary discussants framed the result as the second consecutive negative adjuvant-IO HCC Phase 3 (after IMbrave-050). The pattern argues that the post-resection HCC immune microenvironment is mechanistically distinct from advanced HCC — adjuvant IO does not extrapolate cleanly. Bears directly on perioperative-IO design across other tumor types where similar extrapolations are being tested.

## Practice-changing verdict

**No — preserves SOC.** Active surveillance with imaging remains the post-curative HCC standard. The negative signal is itself practice-relevant: it removes the empirical basis for off-label adjuvant pembrolizumab in HCC.
