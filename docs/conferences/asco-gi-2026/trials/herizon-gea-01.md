# HERIZON-GEA-01 — Zanidatamab + chemo +/- tislelizumab in 1L HER2+ G/GEJ adenocarcinoma

**Headline:** Both zanidatamab arms produced statistically significant and clinically meaningful PFS gains over trastuzumab + chemo in 1L HER2+ locally advanced unresectable or metastatic gastroesophageal adenocarcinoma; the IO-containing arm (zanidatamab + tislelizumab + chemo) added an OS benefit. Practice-changing — trastuzumab + chemo is no longer the SOC reference frame for 1L HER2+ G/GEJ.

## At a glance

- **Sponsor / PI / institution:** Jazz Pharmaceuticals (co-developed with BeiGene for tislelizumab arm); presented by Yelena Janjigian, Memorial Sloan Kettering
- **NCT ID:** NCT05152147
- **Phase / arms / N:** Phase 3, three-arm randomized; zanidatamab + chemo vs zanidatamab + tislelizumab + chemo vs trastuzumab + chemo; ~900 patients globally
- **Primary endpoint(s):** PFS (BICR) and OS, hierarchical
- **Indication / line:** 1L HER2+ (IHC 3+ or IHC 2+/FISH+) locally advanced unresectable or metastatic gastroesophageal adenocarcinoma
- **Comparator:** Trastuzumab + chemo (CAPOX or FP)
- **Session:** Day 3 (Sat Jan 10, 2026), Gastric/GEJ General Session, Moscone West

## Headline result

Both zanidatamab-containing arms showed statistically significant and clinically meaningful PFS improvement vs trastuzumab + chemo; the zanidatamab + tislelizumab + chemo arm additionally demonstrated an OS gain over the trastuzumab + chemo control. Subgroup signal was consistent across PD-L1 CPS strata and HER2 IHC 3+ vs 2+/FISH+. Safety was manageable; no new safety signals beyond known zanidatamab GI/infusion-reaction profile plus tislelizumab IO toxicities.

## Mechanism / class

Zanidatamab is a bispecific HER2-targeted antibody binding two non-overlapping HER2 epitopes (ECD4 + ECD2), driving receptor clustering, internalization and ADCC — distinct from trastuzumab monovalency. Tislelizumab is an anti–PD-1 mAb engineered to minimize Fc-gamma binding.

## Discussant takeaway

Yelena Janjigian framed this as the trastuzumab-displacement moment in 1L HER2+ G/GEJ. The open discussant questions are now: which HER2 agent in 1L vs 2L+ sequencing; and how PD-L1 CPS interacts with the IO-add decision.

## Practice-changing verdict

**Yes.** FDA submission was triggered Q1 2026; NCCN reconsideration expected through 2026. Reshapes 1L SOC reference frame for HER2+ G/GEJ.
