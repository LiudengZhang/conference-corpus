# REFOCUS — Lirafugratinib in FGFR2-fusion cholangiocarcinoma

**Headline:** Lirafugratinib (Relay Therapeutics) delivered durable responses in an ~100-patient Phase 2 of FGFRi-naive FGFR2-fusion intrahepatic cholangiocarcinoma, with a cleaner hyperphosphatemia and ocular-toxicity profile than first-generation pan-FGFR TKIs. Positions the molecule as the best-in-class candidate for next-generation FGFR2-selective TKIs in CCA.

## At a glance

- **Sponsor / PI / institution:** Relay Therapeutics; presented by Lipika Goyal (Stanford) / Milind Javle (MD Anderson) — REFOCUS investigators
- **NCT ID:** NCT05576662
- **Phase / arms / N:** Phase 1/2 single-arm; ~100 evaluable FGFR2-fusion CCA patients in Phase 2 expansion (FGFRi-naive cohort)
- **Primary endpoint(s):** ORR (BICR), DoR, safety
- **Indication / line:** Locally advanced or metastatic FGFR2-fusion+ cholangiocarcinoma, prior systemic therapy, FGFRi-naive
- **Comparator:** None (single-arm); historical reference frame pemigatinib / futibatinib
- **Session:** Day 2 (Fri Jan 9, 2026), Hepatobiliary General Session, Moscone West

## Headline result

Phase 2 expansion showed durable confirmed responses in FGFRi-naive FGFR2-fusion CCA with response rate and duration competitive with first-generation FGFR inhibitors (pemigatinib, futibatinib). Critically, the FGFR2-selective design (sparing FGFR1) yielded markedly lower hyperphosphatemia and ocular AE rates — the dose-limiting toxicities that drive treatment interruption with pan-FGFR TKIs.

## Mechanism / class

Lirafugratinib (RLY-4008) is an irreversible, highly selective FGFR2 inhibitor designed using Relay's motion-based drug design platform to discriminate FGFR2 from FGFR1/3/4. Selectivity avoids the FGFR1-driven hyperphosphatemia and FGFR3/4-driven dermatologic/GI toxicity of pan-FGFR agents.

## Discussant takeaway

Robin Kate Kelley / Ghassan Abou-Alfa anchored the hepatobiliary discussant slot. Framing: FGFR2 CCA is moving from a single first-generation TKI era (pemigatinib / futibatinib) into a class-differentiated era where AE tolerability and resistance-mutation coverage (gatekeeper V564, molecular brake mutations) become the decision axis.

## Practice-changing verdict

**Likely yes, pending regulatory.** Accelerated-approval-grade ORR/DoR in a biomarker-selected niche; class differentiation on tolerability is real. Watch for regulatory submission and head-to-head data vs pemigatinib / futibatinib.
