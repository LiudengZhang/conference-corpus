# ASCO GI 2026

**2026 ASCO Gastrointestinal Cancers Symposium** — Moscone West, San Francisco, CA · January 8 – 10, 2026. ~3,500 in-person attendees + virtual track. Co-sponsored by ASCO, ASTRO, AGA, and SSO; the dominant GI-cancer clinical-readout meeting of the year and a curtain-raiser for the GI track at [ASCO 2026](../asco-2026/index.md) (May 29 – Jun 2, McCormick Place).

> **Status:** Retrospective vault — meeting closed **four months ago** (Jan 8–10, 2026). All abstract embargoes long since lifted; trial pages can cite the ASCO Meeting Library directly. Continuity link forward to the ASCO main meeting matters most here: several of the GI-track LBAs at ASCO 2026 (Rasolute-302 daraxonrasib Plenary; HERIZON-GEA-01 follow-up; BREAKWATER OS update) had their pivotal or precursor readouts at ASCO GI 2026 in January, and the discussant framing from January is the prior the May discussants will be reacting to.

## Why this is in the vault

ASCO GI is the **subspecialty Plenary** for GI oncology — the place pancreatic, CRC, gastric/GEJ, biliary, HCC, esophageal, neuroendocrine, and anal readouts land first. Roughly 700 oral + poster abstracts across three days, with a 4-track plenary-style format (CRC + anal Day 1; pancreatic + small-bowel + neuroendocrine + HCC + biliary Day 2; esophageal + gastric Day 3). The discussant culture is denser than ASCO main meeting — each oral session has a named GI subspecialist framing the data in real time.

The vault's GI sibling structure:

- **ASCO GI (Jan)** — subspecialty readouts. Targeted-therapy biomarker-selected populations (HER2+, FGFR2+, IDH1-mut, BRAF-V600E, MSI-H, KRAS-G12C/G12D, claudin 18.2). Phase 1/2 dose expansion + Phase 2/3 confirmatory.
- **ASCO main meeting (May/Jun)** — the broader GI track, including phase 3 OS confirmations of January's PFS readouts (e.g., Rasolute-302 Plenary LBA5 in May builds on daraxonrasib Phase 1/2 ASCO GI Jan 2025).
- **ESMO GI (Jul)** — European subspecialty follow-up; later in the cycle, often EU-perspective updates.
- **ESMO main meeting (Oct)** — global GI track + late-year OS readouts.

ASCO GI 2026 anchors the January end of this cycle and the discussant framing here propagates through the year.

## Program shape

ASCO GI 2026 ran **Thursday January 8 (opening sessions, CRC + anal) through Saturday January 10 (esophageal + gastric)**, three days, Moscone West.

- **No formal Plenary slot** (unlike ASCO main meeting). Instead, each disease group runs a **General Session** with the highest-impact orals: ~3–5 abstracts framed by a senior discussant, with the day's "headlines" emerging organically from these.
- **Day 1 (Thu Jan 8) — CRC, anal:** CRC General Session anchored by **BREAKWATER cohort 3 OS update** (encorafenib + cetuximab + FOLFIRI in BRAF-V600E mCRC), CIRCULATE-NA ctDNA-guided adjuvant updates, FOxTROT-2 elderly perioperative. Anal cancer: POD1UM-303 / EA2176 retifanlimab maintenance follow-up.
- **Day 2 (Fri Jan 9) — pancreatic, neuroendocrine, hepatobiliary, small-bowel:** **Daraxonrasib + chemo combination updates** (Revolution Medicines, ahead of Plenary LBA5 at ASCO 2026); **RMC-9805 / zoldonrasib** KRAS-G12D PDAC Phase 1 expansion (30% ORR, 80% DCR previously read at ASCO GI 2025; mature follow-up here). HCC: KEYNOTE-937 (adjuvant pembro, negative) + TACTICS-L (atezo-bev vs TACE intermediate-stage HCC). Biliary: REFOCUS (lirafugratinib in FGFR2-fusion CCA, ~100-patient Phase 2, durable responses); IDH1-mutant CCA combination data.
- **Day 3 (Sat Jan 10) — gastric/GEJ, esophageal:** **HERIZON-GEA-01** zanidatamab + chemo ± tislelizumab in 1L HER2+ G/GEJ (Jazz Pharma; positive PFS for both Ziihera arms vs trastuzumab + chemo; OS gain for IO-containing arm — practice-changing). ILUSTRO updates (zolbetuximab claudin 18.2 follow-up). Esophageal: HER2 ADC follow-ups; CheckMate-577 adjuvant nivolumab long-term.

The meeting hit **5,000+ in-person + virtual attendees** (per ASCO press preview), an increase over 2025.

## What's different from ASCO main meeting

- **Smaller, denser, more biomarker-focused.** ~3,500 attendees vs ~40,000. Every abstract has a named biomarker selection criterion in the title — KRAS-G12D, BRAF-V600E, FGFR2-fusion, IDH1-R132, HER2+, MSI-H, claudin 18.2+, RET-fusion, NTRK-fusion. The era of "all-comer chemo trials" is over for GI.
- **Phase 1/2 dose-expansion data is the headline, not Phase 3.** Unlike ASCO main where Plenary is randomized phase 3, ASCO GI's most-discussed abstracts are often **Phase 1 dose-expansion biomarker-defined cohorts** (e.g., RMC-9805 KRAS-G12D PDAC, lirafugratinib FGFR2 CCA, divarasib KRAS-G12C CRC). These define the next year's pivotal designs.
- **Discussant culture is denser.** Three days, ~30 oral sessions, each with a named discussant — discussants here typically frame the field for 6–12 months until ASCO main meeting builds on it.
- **Strong ctDNA / MRD presence.** CIRCULATE-NA, CIRCULATE-Japan, GALAXY, COLOMATE, DYNAMIC follow-ups — ASCO GI is where ctDNA-guided adjuvant trial design gets stress-tested before it migrates to broader oncology.
