# KEYNOTE-A18 mature OS — Pembrolizumab + chemoradiation in high-risk locally advanced cervical cancer

> **Abstract:** Plenary V mature OS · **Session:** Monday April 13, 2026 (cervical Plenary) · **Simultaneous publication:** TBD (likely *JCO* mature OS update)

**Headline:** Long-term OS HR 0.73 with 48-month OS 75.4% vs 70.2% — durable confirmation of pembrolizumab + chemoradiation as 1L SOC for high-risk (stage IB–IVA) locally advanced cervical cancer.

## At a glance

- **Sponsor / cooperative groups:** Merck · ENGOT-cx11 / GOG-3047
- **PI / presenter:** Apr 13 Plenary V (presenter TBD)
- **NCT ID:** NCT04221945
- **Phase / arms / N:** Phase 3 randomized double-blind; 1,060 patients (529 pembro + CCRT vs 531 placebo + CCRT)
- **Primary endpoint(s):** PFS and OS (dual primary); mature OS at SGO 2026
- **Indication / line:** Locally advanced cervical cancer, high-risk (FIGO 2014 Stage IB2–IIB node+ or III–IVA)
- **Comparator:** Placebo + cisplatin-based chemoradiation
- **Session:** Plenary V (Day 4, Apr 13)

## Headline result

Mature OS HR 0.73 (95% CI 0.57–0.94). 36-month OS 81.8% vs 74.4%; 48-month OS 75.4% vs 70.2%. Curves continue to separate over time consistent with durable immune memory. No new safety signals over extended follow-up.

## Mechanism / class

Pembrolizumab is a humanized IgG4 anti–PD-1 monoclonal antibody. KEYNOTE-A18 was the registrational trial for the 2024 FDA approval of pembrolizumab + CCRT in high-risk LACC. The 48-mo mature OS update consolidates the new SOC.

## Discussant takeaway

Plenary V framing: the 48-mo OS curves at 75.4% vs 70.2% represent durable cure-fraction enrichment with an immune-modulated approach in a setting historically defined by chemoradiation alone. Reinforces molecular biomarker (PD-L1) investigation in follow-up analyses.

## Practice-changing verdict

**Yes (confirmatory).** Pembro + CCRT is already 1L SOC for high-risk LACC; mature 48-mo OS data confirm the magnitude and durability of benefit. NCCN cervical guidelines reinforced.
