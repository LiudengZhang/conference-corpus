# BLUESTAR — Puxitatug samrotecan (AZD8205, B7-H4 ADC) in advanced/metastatic endometrial cancer

> **Abstract:** LBA, Phase 1/2a · **Session:** Sunday April 12, 2026 · **Simultaneous publication:** TBD (likely *JCO* or *Annals of Oncology*)

**Headline:** First-in-human B7-H4 ADC clinical efficacy data — ORR 34.6% @ 2.0 mg/kg and 38.5% @ 2.4 mg/kg in endometrial cancer with mPFS 7.0 mo, opening a new ADC target class with read-through to breast and ovarian.

## At a glance

- **Sponsor:** AstraZeneca (AZD8205)
- **PI / presenter:** Stephanie Gaillard, MD (Johns Hopkins)
- **NCT ID:** NCT05123482
- **Phase / arms / N:** Phase 1/2a basket; endometrial cohort dosed at 2.0 mg/kg and 2.4 mg/kg Q3W
- **Primary endpoint(s):** ORR (RECIST 1.1), safety, PK
- **Indication / line:** Advanced/metastatic endometrial cancer, post-platinum and post-IO
- **Comparator:** None (single-arm dose-cohort)
- **Session:** LBA, Day 3 (Apr 12)

## Headline result

In the endometrial cohort, ORR 34.6% at the 2.0 mg/kg dose and 38.5% at 2.4 mg/kg, with 12-week DCR of 80.8% and 84.6% respectively. mPFS 7.0 months. Manageable safety profile consistent with topoisomerase-1 ADC class. First clinical efficacy data for the B7-H4 ADC class.

## Mechanism / class

Puxitatug samrotecan (AZD8205) is an antibody-drug conjugate targeting B7-H4 (VTCN1) — a B7-family immune checkpoint ligand highly expressed in endometrial, breast (TNBC), and ovarian cancer with limited normal-tissue expression. Payload is a proprietary AstraZeneca topoisomerase-1 inhibitor with a cleavable linker. First-in-class B7-H4 ADC to report meaningful clinical efficacy.

## Discussant takeaway

Per OncLive coverage: the 34.6–38.5% ORR in heavily pretreated endometrial sits comparable to current second-line standards and marks B7-H4 as a tractable ADC target. IHC-based patient selection (B7-H4+) is feasible and was used in BLUESTAR.

## Practice-changing verdict

**No (early-phase), but pivotal-design-defining.** BLUESTAR establishes the B7-H4 ADC class clinically and sets the stage for a registration-enabling Phase 3 expansion. AZ presented the basket BLUESTAR design at AACR 2026 (one week after SGO) anchoring ovarian and breast cohorts.
