## Rubric

We score each conference 0–2 on four criteria.

| Criterion | 2 | 1 | 0 |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Axis fit** — extends ≥1 of the AACR axes (agentic AI, single-cell/spatial, virtual cells, bioinfo/AI methods, clinical trials) | multiple axes | one axis | none |
| **Source accessibility** — public material to extract from | full abstracts / transcripts | public coverage only | closed |
| **Signal density** — selectivity / SNR | tight, high per-talk relevance | broad, with relevant sub-tracks | high noise |
| **Adds new vantage** — covers a stage or domain we don't already have | yes, distinct | partial overlap | duplicates existing |

**Build decision:** Total **≥6** → full vault · **4–5** → light scaffold + selected dossiers · **≤3** → skip.

## What we're skipping (and why)

Nothing yet — all 12 cleared the bar at 4+. Two scored "light" rather than "full":

- **ESMO 2026 (5)** — partial overlap with ASCO. We'll pull abstracts but lean on a single landscape page rather than full topic decomposition.
- **NeurIPS 2026 (4)** — methods-relevant but huge and noisy. Filter to ML4Health, Foundation Models for Science, and AI-for-Drug-Discovery workshops only.

Conferences **considered but not added** (would score below 4 against this rubric):

- **HIMSS** — health IT / business, low axis fit
- **AHA Scientific Sessions** — cardiology, low axis overlap
- **AAAI / CVPR / ICML** — methods-relevant but signal-density problem similar to NeurIPS; would duplicate that filter

If the rubric weights ever change (e.g., we start caring about cardio-onc, or open a "platform/biz" axis), this list flips fast.
