# PI3K&#948; in CD4&#8314; T cells protects against lung metastases through IFN&#947;-induced tumor senescence

**Poster #**: 158
**Session**: Immune Cell Biology and Tumor-Immune Crosstalk

**Date**: 4/19/2026 2:00:00 PM

**Authors**: Leqi Tang1, Grace Cooper1, Linsey Porter2, Seung joon Kim3, Erick Armingol4, Oliver Burton1, James  E.  Thaventhiran5, Walid T. Khaled6, Roser Vento-tormo4, Rahul Roychoudhuri1, Klaus Okkenhaug1

1Department of Pathology, University of Cambridge, Cambridge, United Kingdom,2Cambridge Stem Cell Institute, Cambridge, United Kingdom,3University of Oxford, Oxford, United Kingdom,4Wellcome Sanger Institute, Cambridge, United Kingdom,5MRC Toxicology Unit, Cambridge, United Kingdom,6Department of Pharmacology, University of Cambridge, Cambridge, United Kingdom

PI3K&#948; is a key lymphocyte signaling hub for manipulating antitumor immunity. In cancer, most work has centered on inhibiting PI3K&#948;, particularly in regulatory T cells, to relieve immunosuppression and enhance effector T cell control of established tumors. Yet lung metastasis remains a leading cause of cancer mortality, and the impact of PI3K&#948; modulation on metastatic outgrowth remains incompletely understood. Inspired by gain-of-function PIK3CD mutations that cause activated PI3K&#948; syndrome (APDS), we ask whether enhancing PI3K&#948; signaling can be harnessed to prevent melanoma lung metastases. Here, we demonstrate that hyperactivation of PI3K&#948; confers robust protection against pulmonary melanoma metastases in a CD4+ T cell - dependent manner that drives IFN&#947;-mediated tumor cell senescence. 
We employed mice with germline or conditional hyperactive PI3K&#948;, challenged intravenously with B16F10 melanoma to establish lung metastasis. Cell-specific Cre systems identified critical immune populations mediating metastasis rejection. Single-cell RNA-seq and spectral cytometry defined immune remodeling, while depleting and neutralizing antibodies as well as CRISPR-edited melanoma lines revealed underlying mechanism. 
Germline hyperactive-PI3K&#948; mice developed markedly fewer lung metastases than wild-type controls, and inducible activation of hyperactive PI3K&#948; in the hematopoietic compartment was sufficient to confer protection. Protection was dependent on CD4+ T cells - CD4+ T cell specific PI3K&#948; hyperactivation recapitulated germline protection, while B cell, NK cell and CD8+ T cell specific PI3K&#948; hyperactivation did not. Antibody depletions confirmed that CD4+ depletion abolished protection while CD8+ depletion had no effect. Hyperactive PI3K&#948; CD4+ T cells showed enhanced Th1 differentiation with increased IFN&#947; and IFN&#947;-neutralization eliminated the protection against metastasis. Mechanistically, tumor control required IFNGR1/JAK1 signaling in cancer cells but not TNFR1, and operated independent of tumor MHC-I or MHC-II expression. The protective mechanism converged on IFN&#947;-induced tumor senescence, as lung metastases from hyperactive-PI3K&#948; mice displayed increased SA-&#946;-gal activity, indicating that CD4+ T cells halt metastatic progression by enforcing tumor cell senescence. 
Our findings reveal a novel protective axis where hyperactive PI3K&#948; signaling in CD4+ T cells drives a type 1-skewed pulmonary anti-metastasis microenvironment. This elevates CD4+ T cells from &#34;helpers&#34; to anti-metastatic effectors using IFN&#947;-dependent tumor-intrinsic senescence to achieve durable immune control. These results reveal hyperactive PI3K&#948; as a double-edged sword: while pathogenic in APDS, it reinforces immune surveillance of metastasis, offering new therapeutic avenues for preventing metastatic progression by enhancing PI3K&#948; signaling.
