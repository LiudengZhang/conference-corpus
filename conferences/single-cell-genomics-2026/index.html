    Single-Cell Genomics 2026¶

    The two flagship single-cell community meetings of 2026, captured under one vault. The brand "Single Cell Genomics" splits across two events this year and both are in-scope for the AACR cross-link:

    1. Gordon Research Conference on Single-Cell Genomics 2026 — Les Diablerets, Switzerland, May 10–15, 2026. Chair: Angela Oliveira Pisco. Subtitled "Accelerating Medical Discovery With Single Cell Omics and Machine Learning." This is the de-facto annual Single-Cell Genomics anchor for the field (alternates years with the Single-Cell Cancer Biology GRC, also at Les Diablerets). The associated GRS (graduate-student/postdoc seminar) runs May 9–10 on "Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning for Single-Cell and Spatial Omics." Oversubscribed as of May 2026.
    2. Wellcome Connecting Science — Single Cell Biology 2026 — Wellcome Genome Campus, Hinxton, UK, June 10–12, 2026. Committee: Leeat Keren (Weizmann), Cole Trapnell (UW), Roser Vento-Tormo (Sanger). Keynote: Ido Amit (Weizmann). Hashtag #SingleCell26. In-person + virtual hybrid; abstract deadline was Mar 30, in-person registration closes May 11.

    Status: Scaffold — both meetings upcoming or active. GRC 2026 is happening this week (the vault is being built mid-meeting, May 11, 2026). Wellcome Single Cell Biology drops a month later. Full program for the Wellcome meeting was released April 22, 2026; the GRC program is closed-door per Gordon Conference policy (no recording, no public talk titles, attendees-only).

    Brand-disambiguation up front: there is no single meeting in 2026 literally titled "Single Cell Genomics" hosted by EMBL or the Sanger Institute. EMBL Heidelberg's last "Single-Cell Omics" conference ran in 2022 (sic22-01) and is not on the 2026 calendar — EMBL's 2026 single-cell-adjacent slot is the Brain (epi)genome symposium (Apr 21–24, in-house single-cell + 3D-genomics axis). The two events above are the two real 2026 flagships for the single-cell community. The CSHL "Single Cell Analyses" meeting alternates years and is not running in 2026 either; its sibling slot at CSHL is the Biological Data Science meeting (see cshl-bds-2026/), which has spatial/single-cell as one of six sessions but is not a single-cell-focused meeting per se.

    Why this is in the vault¶

    • Highest single-cell methods density of any meeting in the corpus. GRC + Wellcome Single Cell Biology together are where the next generation of scRNA / scATAC / spatial / multimodal tools are first presented, in front of an audience of ~200 (GRC) + ~250 (Wellcome) of the field's tightest-knit community. Tool density per attendee is higher than RECOMB, ISMB, or EuroBioC — every oral talk is a single-cell method, dataset, or atlas.
    • Direct AACR-axis fit on the single-cell + spatial bin. The AACR 2026 single-cell + spatial topic (aacr-2026/topics/single-cell-spatial-omics/) is the largest topic folder in the AACR vault — 20 session transcripts, ~273k words, 1,015 poster abstracts. Almost every analytical method cited in those AACR talks was originally presented at a Single-Cell Genomics GRC, the Wellcome meeting, or the older EMBL Single-Cell Omics line. Tracking these two 2026 meetings catches the upstream-tool previews of what will surface at AACR 2027.
    • Foundation-model-era axis. The GRC 2026 subtitle ("Single Cell Omics and Machine Learning") and the GRS topic ("AI and ML for Single-Cell and Spatial Omics") explicitly center the scGPT / Geneformer / scFoundation wave that AACR 2026 only touched in passing. Charlotte Bunne (EPFL, Wellcome speaker) is a key foundation-models-for-perturbations voice — direct overlap with the AACR agentic-AI dossier and the RECOMB 2026 / ISMB 2026 MLCSB tracks.
    • Lineage tracing + perturbation atlases. Wellcome speakers James Gagnon (Utah) and Theresa Loveless (Rice) are at the front of recording-based lineage tracing; this is where the EMBO Lineage Tracing 2026 meeting (Mar 2026) handed off its momentum. GRC will likely cover Perturb-seq successors, CRISPR screen single-cell readouts.
    • Spatial-transcriptomics anchor. Mats Nilsson (Stockholm; ISS / in-situ-sequencing), Sizun Jiang (Harvard; CODEX/Akoya), and Roser Vento-Tormo (Sanger; tissue atlases) at Wellcome plus the GRC "Spatial and Proteomics Analysis" session cover the same Visium-HD / Xenium / CosMx / MERFISH substrate that drives the AACR spatial-biomarkers sessions.

    Program shape¶

    GRC SCG 2026 (May 10–15, Les Diablerets)¶

    The GRC's published session structure (7 themed sessions across 5 days):

    • AI for Genomics — foundation models, deep-learning embeddings, transfer learning across atlases
    • Transcription Factor Regulation — TF-binding inference from scATAC, GRN reconstruction
    • Single-Cell Modeling — perturbation-response models, optimal transport, lineage dynamics
    • Applications in Medicine — disease atlases, clinical biomarkers, drug response
    • Spatial and Proteomics Analysis — Visium HD / Xenium / CODEX / CITE-seq, spatial-graph methods
    • 3D Genomics — Hi-C, scHi-C, single-cell 3D chromatin
    • Epigenomics — scATAC, single-cell methylation, multimodal chromatin

    GRC pattern is single-track, ~5 talks/session, late-afternoon discussion + poster sessions, conferee count capped at ~200. Expected: 30–40 oral talks + 2 keynote slots + ~120 posters.

    Wellcome Single Cell Biology 2026 (Jun 10–12, Hinxton)¶

    Six published session topics over 2.5 days:

    • Single-cell going large scale — atlas-scale datasets, sample multiplexing, cost-down workflows
    • Beyond transcriptomics — proteomics (CITE-seq successors), single-cell metabolomics, multi-omics
    • Non-model organisms — single-cell in non-mouse/human (Mara Lawniczak — malaria parasite, mosquito atlases)
    • Technology development — new chemistry, new hardware, miniaturization (BD Rhapsody / 10x next-gen / Parse Bio / Singleron / Honeycomb)
    • Computational approaches — foundation models, integration, perturbation modeling, lineage inference
    • Spatial technologies — Mats Nilsson (ISS), Sizun Jiang (CODEX), Vento-Tormo (tissue atlases)

    Hybrid format. Bursaries available for trainees + LMIC delegates. Posters can be virtual.

    Confirmed speakers (Wellcome) as of Apr 22, 2026: Ido Amit (keynote, Weizmann), Charlotte Bunne (EPFL), Gonçalo Castelo-Branco (Karolinska), Giovanni D'Angelo (EPFL), James Gagnon (Utah — lineage tracing), Sizun Jiang (Harvard — spatial proteomics), Leeat Keren (Weizmann, organizer), Mara Lawniczak (Sanger — non-model), Theresa Loveless (Rice — recording lineage), Heather Marlow (Chicago), Mats Nilsson (Stockholm — ISS), Mor Nitzan (Hebrew U — theory), Cole Trapnell (UW, organizer — Monocle / sci-RNA-seq lineage), Céline Vallot (Curie — single-cell epigenomics in cancer), Roser Vento-Tormo (Sanger, organizer — tissue atlases / cell-cell communication).

    What's different from EuroBioC / CSHL BDS / RECOMB / ISMB¶

    • GRC is closed-door. Gordon Conference rules: no recording, no live-tweeting, no public talk titles, attendees-only press. Even the speaker list isn't published in advance. The vault for the GRC half of this meeting builds entirely post-hoc from (a) preprints that drop in the weeks after the meeting and reference "presented at GRC SCG 2026," (b) Bluesky/X posts from attendees with the chair's blessing, (c) the Pisco lab's eventual recap if she writes one. This is the opposite of the EuroBioC / GBCC / Nextflow Summit pattern (full recordings, slides on Zenodo).
    • Wellcome is hybrid, partially recorded. Keynotes usually go to YouTube. Speaker bios + titles are public. Closer to the EuroBioC / GBCC information model.
    • Two-meeting compound vault. Unlike other conference vaults (one meeting per vault), this one bridges two events that share a community and a sub-discipline. The digest/ folder is split with grc-* and wellcome-* prefixes. The tools/ index treats them as a single 2026 single-cell-methods cohort — many tools will be presented at both, and the tool-page Talk field becomes Talk at GRC + Talk at Wellcome as separate rows.
    • No industry track. Unlike AGBT or JPM, neither meeting has a paid sponsor track. 10x / Parse / Singleron / Element / Curio Bio / Vizgen / NanoString reps are in the audience and at the bar but don't get slots. Platform-launch news happens in side-conversations and shows up in trade-press writeups later.
    • The "AI/ML" turn is explicit. GRC 2026 is the first Single-Cell Genomics GRC to put AI/ML in the conference subtitle. This formalizes a shift that started at GRC 2024 (Zhuang/Fan/Xie/Pisco co-organized) and is now centerpiece. Foundation-model talks expected in 3 of 7 GRC sessions (AI for Genomics, Single-Cell Modeling, parts of Applications in Medicine).
    • High preprint ratio post-meeting. Empirically ~75–85% of GRC SCG talks have a bioRxiv preprint within 6 months of the meeting (compare ~85–90% at CSHL methods meetings, ~60% at AACR). Wellcome's ratio is similar.
