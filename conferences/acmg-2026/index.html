    ACMG 2026¶

    2026 ACMG Annual Clinical Genetics Meeting — Baltimore Convention Center, Baltimore, MD · March 10 – 14, 2026. ~3,500 attendees — the largest US clinical-genetics meeting, hosted by the American College of Medical Genetics and Genomics. Meeting theme: Where Genetics Meets Innovation. Presidential Plenary theme: The Future of Expanded Newborn Genomic Screening.

    Status: Scaffold (post-meeting) — ready for retrospective bulk extraction. Meeting concluded ~8 weeks ago (March 10–14, 2026) as of today (May 11, 2026). The structure lands now so the platform-presentation slate, ACMG/AMP variant-classification updates, and the genomic-oncology / hereditary-cancer subset can be pulled from the abstract supplement (Genetics in Medicine / GIM Open) and the on-demand digital edition. Anything in this vault tagged *per pre-meeting press / not yet reconciled with abstract supplement* is sourced from the ACMG meeting site and ACMG Foundation press releases; titles and assignments will be reconciled when per-abstract DOIs land.

    Why this is in the vault¶

    ACMG is the clinical-genetics decision-rules venue. Where ASHG runs the methods and population-genetics framing, ACMG runs the clinical-laboratory implementation: who orders the test, how is the variant classified, what does the report say, what does the clinician do with it. The overlap with the AACR / ASCO oncology cycle is concentrated but high-value:

    1. Hereditary-cancer panels and germline-somatic integration. ACMG runs the ACMG Secondary Findings list (most recent: v3.3, with continued debate over scope and implementation). Panel-classification accuracy, universal-testing thresholds, and pediatric-cancer-predisposition screening all live here. Same investigators show up at AACR with somatic-overlay analyses 4–6 weeks later.
    2. ACMG/AMP variant-classification standards. The ACMG/AMP 2015 standards and their successor SVI / ClinGen recalibrations (PS3/BS3 functional evidence, BA1 / BS1 allele-frequency thresholds, AlphaMissense-as-PP3 controversy) are debated and updated here. Every clinical-genetics laboratory in the US uses this framework. Cancer-variant classification rests on it.
    3. Clonal hematopoiesis and somatic mosaicism in cancer testing. ACMG 2026 ran a dedicated session "Clonal Hematopoiesis, Somatic and Post-Zygotic Mosaicism in Multigene Cancer Testing for Geneticists" — the clinical-lab translation of the population-scale CHIP findings that anchor ASHG / ASH talks.
    4. AI in clinical genomics. ACMG 2026 platform presentations included "Demystifying Base Large Language Model Reproducibility and Accuracy in ACMG/AMP Variant Classification" and AI-assisted genome interpretation in lab workflows. This is the clinical-lab-validation venue for foundation-model variant-effect predictors (AlphaMissense, ESM, EVO follow-ons) that debut at ISMB / ASHG.
    5. Newborn screening expansion to genomic sequencing. Presidential Plenary theme. Cross-cuts cancer predisposition (RB1, TP53, RET, APC, BRCA, Lynch) via the secondary-findings debate — should pediatric cancer-predisposition variants be reported from newborn WGS?

    Hybrid template (and the design decision behind this vault): ACMG is neither pure-clinical-readout (ASCO / SABCS) nor pure-methods (ISMB / RECOMB) — most platform slots are "clinical-lab investigator anchored on a cohort + classification framework + lab implementation," with a substantial AI/ML methods overlay and a strong case-based-session thread. The closest sibling shape in the vault is ASHG 2026, which collapses to a single talks/ directory with a per-talk hybrid template; we apply the same shape here, with the clinical-lab / cancer-genetics / variant-classification subset prioritized.

    • Wed Mar 11 – Sat Mar 14: Annual Meeting proper. Presidential Plenary + parallel platform sessions + concurrent symposia + poster halls + case-based sessions + TED-style talks.

    • Abstract scale: ~1,500+ accepted abstracts (per recent-year ACMG pattern; 2026 exact count to be confirmed); ~150+ platform / featured slots; ~1,200+ posters across multiple sessions.

    • Health Services / Implementation / ELSI — return of results, payer coverage, LDT regulation, equity / access.
    • Education, Training, Workforce — genetic-counseling workforce, residency curricula, fellowship.

    What's different from sibling meetings¶

    • Clinical-lab orientation. Unlike ASHG (population genetics + methods) or AACR (cancer biology + trials), ACMG is the meeting where the clinical laboratory directors (FACMG-certified) and clinical geneticists (board-certified medical geneticists) meet. The questions are: is this variant pathogenic, what does the report say, what does the clinician do with it.
    • Standards-document venue. ACMG and ClinGen working groups release updates at this meeting — variant-classification standards, secondary-findings list revisions, gene-disease validity curations, prenatal sequencing position statements. These are practice-defining documents.
    • Smaller and more focused than ASHG. ~3,500 attendees vs ASHG's ~7,500; tighter program (~1,500 abstracts vs ASHG's ~3,000); deeper clinical-lab content density.
    • Hybrid clinical + research format. Many talks are clinical case readouts, not research presentations. The case-based session is a load-bearing slot type. CME / P.A.C.E. / NSGC credit programming dominates a meaningful fraction of session-hours.
    • Industry-press density. Higher than ASHG because every major clinical-genetics-lab vendor (Illumina, Baylor Genetics, GeneDx, Natera, Ambry, Myriad, Quest, LabCorp Diagnostics, Tempus, Caris) presents data and runs sponsor symposia.
    • March timing. ACMG is the first big genomics meeting of the year after JPM — clinical-lab pipelines that get pitched at JPM (January) often have their first peer-reviewed-style readout at ACMG (March), then a methods-paper at ASHG (October).
    • Open-access disposition. GIM is hybrid (most content paywalled, growing open-access); GIM Open is fully OA. Citability is moderate; trade-press recaps (GenomeWeb / 360Dx) often the most accessible source pre-publication.

    Cross-vault links¶

    • ASHG 2026 — the methods + population-genetics sibling. Many ACMG investigators have ASHG talks in October with the research-methods framing of the clinical-lab work shown at ACMG.
    • AACR 2026 — cancer-biology / hereditary-cancer overlap. ACMG cancer-genetics platform sessions feed forward to AACR hereditary-cancer / germline-somatic posters 4–6 weeks later.
    • ASCO 2026 — clinical translation. Cancer-predisposition testing, germline-informed PARP / PD-1 trial eligibility, Lynch-syndrome surveillance — methods that mature at ACMG arrive at ASCO with patient outcomes.
    • ASH 2026 — clonal hematopoiesis bridge. CHIP / CCUS / germline-MN-predisposition is a shared topic; ACMG runs the clinical-lab classification framing, ASH runs the clinical sequelae.
    • ISMB 2026 / RECOMB 2026 — methods overlap. AlphaMissense, ESM-genomic, EVO, and DNA-language-model variant-effect predictors that debut at ISMB get clinical-lab-validation discussion at ACMG.
    • AGBT 2026 — platform-launch overlap. Long-read clinical sequencing (PacBio HiFi, ONT R10) clinical implementation discussion at ACMG follows AGBT February platform talks.
    • ESHG 2026 — European clinical-genetics sibling meeting; many overlapping speakers and topics, six weeks later (May/June).
    • JPM 2026 — financing layer for the clinical-lab vendors who show data at ACMG.
    • Conferences timeline — ACMG's position in the year's genomics cycle (JPM Jan → AGBT Feb → ACMG Mar → AACR Apr → ASCO May/Jun → ESHG Jun → ISMB Jul → ESMO/ASHG Oct → SITC Nov → ASH Dec → SABCS Dec).
